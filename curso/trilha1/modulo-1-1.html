🔑 Selling is not pushing

The first step to becoming a professional salesperson is understanding thatselling is not pushing product. Selling is identifying a real problem that the customer has and offering a genuine solution to that problem. When you change this perspective, everything changes: the way you approach, how you listen, how you present and how you close.

What changes in practice?

  • ✓ You stop talking about the product and start asking about the problem
  • ✓ The customer stops feeling pressured and starts to feel understood
  • ✓ The conversation becomes a consultancy, not a sales pitch
  • ✓ The closing rate rises because the offer fits the real need

✓ Professional Mindset

  • "How can I help this customer?"
  • "What's his real problem?"
  • "Does my solution fit here?"
  • “How can I generate value first?”

✗ Amateur Mentality

  • "I need to reach my goal"
  • "I'm going to push this product"
  • "If I talk enough, he buys"
  • "Low price solves everything"

💡 Practical Exercise

Think about the last time you bought something with satisfaction. Did the seller try to push or did he understand your need? Now think: how can you replicate this experience for your customers?

🧭 The difference between convincing and leading

There is a fundamental difference betweenconvince e to drive. Convincing presupposes that the customer is against you and you need to change their mind. Leading assumes that the customer has a problem and you will guide them to the best solution — which may or may not be yours.

Convince vs Lead

✗

Convince

Force, pressure, excessive argumentation. The client feels that they are being manipulated. Even if you buy it, you may regret it.

✓

To drive

Questions, listening, presentation of value. The client feels that he made the decision alone. Buy with confidence and recommend.

📊 Perspective

Research shows that led customers have3x more likely to refer e 2x more likely to buy againthan convinced customers. Driving generates a virtuous cycle of sales.

💎 The role of value before price

When the customer perceives the value before hearing the price, the equation changes completely. Price stops being a barrier and becomes ajustified investment. Value is the perception that the solution solves a problem that costs more than the asking price.

💡 Rule of Thumb

Never reveal price before building value. The correct sequence is:Problem → Consequence → Solution → Result → Price. When the price appears at the end, it appears small compared to the constructed value.

Topic 4

🩺 The seller as a solver

Think of the salesperson as a doctor. A good doctornever prescribe without first diagnosing. He asks questions, examines, understands the history, and only then recommends treatment. The professional salesperson does exactly the same thing: first understands, then offers.

The Salesperson-Doctor Process

1

Anamnesis (Questions)

"What's been going on? How long has it been? What have you tried?"

2

Diagnosis (Understanding)

"I understand. Your problem is X, caused by Y, with consequence Z."

3

Prescription (Solution)

"To solve this, I recommend A, which works by doing B, with result C."

📊 Why does this work?

When you diagnose before prescribing, the client feels that the solution wascustom made. This eliminates the feeling of a "generic product" and creates the perception of personalized consultancy — even if the product is the same for everyone.

⚖️ Consultative selling vs transactional selling

There are two major sales models:transactional(fast, focused on price and convenience) and theconsultative(based on relationship, diagnosis and solution). Neither is better than the other — each serves a context.

Transactional Selling

  • → Simple product, low price
  • → Quick decision, little risk
  • → Focus on convenience and price
  • → Example: buying a coffee, gasoline

Consultative Selling

  • → Complex solution, high ticket
  • → Delayed decision, high risk
  • → Focus on value and relationships
  • → Example: B2B software, consultancy

💡 When to use each model?

Simple rule: the larger themedium ticket, theperceived riskand thecomplexity of the solution, the more consultative the sale should be. The mistake is trying to sell a R$10 product consultatively or a R$100,000 project transactionally.
