🧠 The 5 levels of consumer awareness

Based on Eugene Schwartz's framework, understanding where the customer is in the purchasing journey completely changes how you communicate.

Topic 1
1

😶 Totally unconscious

At the most basic level, the consumerYou don't even know you have a problem. He lives his routine normally, without realizing that something could be different or better. Trying to sell to someone at this level is like offering an umbrella to someone who can't see that it's going to rain.

Practical example

A restaurant owner who loses 30% of his customers due to slow service, but thinks that "it's just like that". He doesn't realize that there is a problem — so he doesn't look for any solution.

How to approach:Educational content that reveals the problem. "Did you know that 30% of customers don't come back because of waiting time?" — now he begins to understand.

💡 Strategy

For the unconscious public,never talk about your product. Talk about the problem he hasn't yet noticed. Use data, statistics and stories that make him think: "Could this happen to me?"

Topic 2
2

😟 Aware of the problem

Here the consumerYou know you have a pain, but you don't know the solution. He feels the discomfort, recognizes that something isn't right, but doesn't know what to do about it. It's the moment when he starts researching, but still without a clear direction.

What does this consumer think

"My sales team doesn't hit targets, but I don't know why..."

"I lose a lot of customers, but I don't know what I'm doing wrong..."

"I know I need to improve, but where do I start?"

📊 Strategy for this level

Offercontent that names the problemand shows that there are paths. Articles like "5 reasons why your team doesn't reach goals" work perfectly. You position yourself as someone who understands pain.

Topic 3
3

🔍 Aware of the solution

The consumer alreadyKnow that there are solutions to your problem, but is still evaluating which path to take. He researches alternatives, compares approaches and tries to understand which method makes the most sense for his reality.

The moment of differentiation

  • ✓ Show why your approach is different (not better — different)
  • ✓ Explain your “unique mechanism” — how you solve the problem
  • ✓ Use cases and testimonials from those who used your approach
  • ✓ Compare (non-competing) methodologies in an educational way

💡 Practical Tip

Ideal content for this level:"Why method X doesn't work for Y and what to do instead". You educate while positioning your approach as the most appropriate.

Topic 4
4

📦 Product Aware

The consumeralready know your product or serviceand is comparing with alternatives. He knows what you do, but he needs specific reasons to choose you over your competitor. It's time for proof, guarantees and details.

What convinces at this level

⭐
Testimonials
Real customer results
🛡️
Guarantees
Risk reduction
🎥
Demonstrations
See it working
📊
Comparisons
Clear differences

📊 Common error at this level

Continue educating when the client already wants to decide. At this point, he doesn't need more educational content — he needsevidence, details and security to make the decision.

Topic 5
5

🔥 Very conscious

The consumerHave you already decided that you want to buy. He knows you, trusts you, and just needs one final push. It could be a special offer, a deadline, a bonus, or simply someone saying: "Shall we do it?"

✓ What works

  • "Offer valid until Friday"
  • "Exclusive bonus for those who close today"
  • "There are 3 spaces left in this lot"
  • "Shall we get started? What is the best form of payment?"

✗ What doesn't work

  • Continue explaining what he already knows
  • Send more educational content
  • Wait for him to take the initiative
  • Being afraid to ask for closure

💡 The golden rule

At this level,the hard work has already been done. All you need is to give the customer a reason to act now instead of later. Real urgency, not artificial. And ask for closure with confidence.

Topic 6
6

💡 Why this changes everything in communication

Using the same message across all levels is the biggest waste in sales and marketing.Each level of consciousness requires a different language, tone and type of argument. When you align the message to the level, conversion skyrockets.

Communication map by level

Unconscious → Educate about the problem with data and stories
Problem → Name the pain and show that there are ways
Solution → Differentiate your approach from alternatives
Product → Prove with testimonials, cases and guarantees
Very consc. → Direct offer with urgency and clear CTA

📊 Real impact

Companies that segment communication by level of awareness reportup to 3x more conversationwith the same budget. It's not about spending more — it's about saying the right thing to the right person at the right time.

Final Summary

📝Module Summary

What you learned:

  • ✓ The 5 levels: unconscious, problem, solution, product, very conscious
  • ✓ Each level requires different communication
  • ✓ Don't try to sell to someone who hasn't noticed the problem yet.
  • ✓ Don't educate those who are ready to buy
  • ✓ Aligning message to level multiplies conversion
  • ✓ Eugene Schwartz framework applied to sales
