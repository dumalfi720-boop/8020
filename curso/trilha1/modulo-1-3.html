💎 Building value before price

Master the art of making the customer perceive value before knowing how much it costs. When the value is clear, the price justifies itself.

Topic 1

📊 The 70% rule

Purchasing behavior research consistently shows that7 out of 10 consumers accept paying more for a product or service when they perceive clear and differentiated value. This means that price is rarely the real barrier — the lack of perceived value is what kills the sale.

What does this mean in practice

70%
Pay more for clear value
vs
30%
They decide only by price

💡 Direct implication

If 70% of customers accept paying more for value,Why are you competing on price?The answer: because you are not building enough value before presenting the price. This is correctable.

Topic 2
2

⚖️ Perceived value vs real price

Every purchasing decision comes down to a simple equation:Perceived Value vs Price. If the customer realizes that the value is greater than the price, they buy. If you realize that the price is greater than the value, you back off. Your job is to ensure that the value side always wins.

The equation in practice

Sale happens:"This software saves my team 20h/month (R$8,000 in salaries). It costs R$500/month. It's an obvious investment."

Sale does not happen:"This software costs R$500/month. It has many features." — Value was not built. The price seems expensive without reference.

📊 Insight

The price never changes. What changes andvalue perception. R$500 may seem expensive or cheap depending on what the customer understands they will get in return. Your job is to build that perception.

Topic 3
3

⏱️ When price should enter the conversation

Price reveal timing is one of the most impactful skills in sales.Revealing too soon kills the salebecause the customer does not yet have a value reference. Revealing too late creates distrust. The sweet spot is right after the customer understands and feels the value.

The ideal sequence

1

Identify the problem

"Tell me: what is the biggest challenge today?"

2

Amplify the consequence

"If this continues for another 6 months, how much will it cost?"

3

Present the solution and the result

"Our customers solve this in X days and save Y per month"

4

Only now: reveal the investment

"Investment is Z — a fraction of what you save"

Topic 4
4

🏗️ How to build value before revealing the price

Building value is a deliberate process, not something that “happens.” There is apractice sequencethat works in any context: stacking layers of value until the price seems small in comparison.

The Value Stack

  • ✓Layer 1:Key result — what the customer will get
  • ✓Layer 2:Time saved — much faster than doing it alone
  • ✓Layer 3:Mistakes avoided — the cost of mistakes he won't make
  • ✓Layer 4:Bonuses and support — everything that comes together
  • ✓Layer 5:Guarantee — total risk reduction

💡 Practical Exercise

Build your product's value stack: list the main result, time saved, errors avoided, bonuses included and the guarantee.Add everything up in reais. The total value must be at least 5x the price you charge.

Topic 5
5

⚔️ The mistake of competing on price

Compete on price and arace to the bottom. There will always be someone willing to charge less. When you enter this war, you lose margin, you lose quality and you lose good customers — because customers who only buy based on price are the ones who complain the most and refer the least.

✗ Compete on price

  • → Increasingly smaller margins
  • → Customers who do not value
  • → Impossibility of investing in quality
  • → Constant and unsustainable stress

✓ Compete on value

  • → Healthy margins
  • → Customers who refer and return
  • → Ability to invest and improve
  • → Long-term sustainable business

📊 Market reference

Apple, Nespresso, Tesla —none of these brands are the cheapest on the market. They are all the most desired. The difference? They have built perceived value so high that price becomes irrelevant.

Topic 6
6

🏢 Examples of value construction in different segments

Value construction works inany segment, but the way of communicating changes. See how to adapt the principle to different market realities.

Value per segment

SaaS/Software

"Our customers reduce churn by 23% in the first 90 days. With your average ticket, this represents R$15,000/month recovered."

Consulting / Services

"Companies that implement this process save an average of 120 hours per quarter. How much is 1 hour worth for your team?"

Retail / E-commerce

"This fabric lasts 3x longer than normal. Instead of buying 3 shirts a year, you buy 1 that lasts 3 years. In the end, it's cheaper."

Infoproducts / Education

"Our students increase revenue by an average of 40% in 6 months. The course pays for itself in the first month of application."

Write 3 value-building sentences for your product/service using the format:"Our clients [result] in [deadline]. This represents [value in reais/hours/percentage]."
