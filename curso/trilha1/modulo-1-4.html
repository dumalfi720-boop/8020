🩺 Why ask before bidding

Imagine a doctor who prescribes medicine before asking how you feel. Absurd, right? In sales, most do exactly this:offers before understanding. Asking before offering is the basis of all consultative selling. And what separates the professional seller from the amateur.

What changes when you ask first

  • ✓You discover what really matters to the customer
  • ✓Your offer perfectly fits the need
  • ✓Objections decrease because the customer feels heard
  • ✓The customer makes the sale for you by verbalizing their own pain

💡 Rule of thumb

In the first 60% of the sales conversation,the customer should talk more than you. If you're talking more, you're selling it wrong.

Topic 2
2

🔑 The 4 questions that change everything

They exist4 universal questionsthat work in any sales context. Memorizing and practicing these questions already puts you ahead of 90% of salespeople.

The 4 Essential Questions

1. "What bothers you?"

Reveal the pain. The client verbalizes the problem in their own words.

2. "How much does this cost you?"

Quantifies the pain. Transform feelings into numbers. Creates value reference.

3. "What happens if nothing changes?"

Creates urgency. The client projects the consequences of inaction. Amplifies the pain.

4. "What would be ideal?"

Reveal the desire. The client describes the perfect scenario — and you can show that you deliver exactly that.

Topic 3

🔥 Questions that magnify pain

Implication questions are the most powerful in SPIN Selling. They make the customercalculate the real cost of not solving the problem. It's not manipulation — it's clarity. You are helping the client see what they already know but have not quantified.

Examples of implication questions

  • →"If this continues for another 6 months, what will be the impact on revenue?"
  • →"How many hours a week does your team lose to this problem?"
  • →"Does this problem affect other areas of the company as well?"
  • →"What's the cost of losing another customer because of this?"

📊 Important information

In original SPIN Selling research (35,000+ sales conversations),implication questions were the #1 factor that differentiated high-performing salespeopleof the medians.

Topic 4
4

🌟 Questions that reveal desire

After increasing the pain, it's time to make the clientvisualize the ideal scenario. Need-for-a-solution questions create an emotional bridge between the current problem and the desired future. The client stops thinking about costs and starts thinking about investment.

Desire Question Examples

"If you could solve this, what would your team's day-to-day life be like?"

"Imagine if this problem no longer existed. What would change?"

"How much would it be worth to you to get those 20 hours a week back?"

💡 Why it works

People buy on emotion and justify it with logic. When the customervisualize and feel the desired future, the desire to get there outweighs any price objections.

⚠️ Common mistakes when asking

Asking is an art, and like all art, it hascommon mistakes that should be avoided. The difference between a good diagnosis and an uncomfortable interrogation lies in the form, tone and rhythm of the questions.

✗ Frequent errors

  • Asking without hearing the answer
  • Ask 10 questions in a row without commenting
  • Too many closed questions (yes/no)
  • Police interrogation tone
  • Not connecting an answer with the next question

✓ Good practices

  • Ask, listen, validate, then ask again
  • Maximum 2-3 questions before a comment
  • Open-ended questions that invite narrative
  • Conversation tone between friends
  • "You mentioned X — tell me more about it"

💡 Practical Exercise

In the next sales conversation,record audio (with permission)and then listen. Count how many times you asked vs how many times you talked about the product. The ideal ratio is 70% questions/listening, 30% presentation.
