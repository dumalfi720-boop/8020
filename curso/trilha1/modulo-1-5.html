📐 Feature vs benefit vs impact

They existthree levels of communicationabout any product: what it HAS (feature), what it DOES (benefit) and what it CHANGES in the customer’s life (impact). Most sellers stop at the first level. The best arrive in third.

The 3 levels in practice

C

Feature (what it HAS)

"Our CRM has integration with WhatsApp"

B

Benefit (what it DOES)

"You respond to customers directly through the CRM without changing screens"

I

Impact (what CHANGES)

"Your salespeople respond 3x faster and no leads are lost — this could mean 30% more sales per month"

2

🎯 The impact formula

Useimpact verbsto transform any characteristic into a tangible result. The formula is simple: start the sentence with "This reduces...", "This speeds up...", "This prevents...", "This increases...".

Impact verbs in action

⬇️
REDUCE
"This reduces response time by 70%"
⚡
ACCELERATE
"This accelerates closing by 5 days"
🛡️
AVOID
“This prevents the loss of hot leads”
📈
INCREASES
"This increases conversion by 25%"
3

🚀 Go from features to results

The customer does not buy features — they buytransformation. Instead of “our software has 47 features,” say “our customers save 12 hours a week.” The result is what matters. Features are a means, not an end.

✗ Feature language

  • "We have a real-time dashboard"
  • "Our plan includes 5 users"
  • "24/7 chat support"
  • "Integration with 200+ tools"

✓ Result language

  • "You see in real time where you are losing money"
  • "Your entire sales team connected in one place"
  • "When a problem arises at 3 am, someone will help you"
  • "Works with everything you already use — without changing anything"
4

🏢 Value language by segment

Each market has itsOwn KPIs and language. Saying "reduces churn" for those in retail doesn't make sense. Adapting the impact language to the segment's vocabulary is essential.

Language by market

SaaS Churn, MRR, CAC, LTV, onboarding, trial conversion
Retail Average ticket, stock turnover, seasonality, loyalty
Services Rework, SLA, satisfaction, retention, referral
Industry OEE, downtime, cost per unit, lead time, waste

📝 Practical translation examples

See thefull translationof characteristics for impact in different contexts. Notice how the same information gains completely different weight.

"We have email automation"

"Your follow-ups happen on their own — no lead goes cold by forgetting"

"Platform with advanced reports"

"You find out in 2 minutes which seller needs help and which campaign is producing results"

"12 month warranty"

"If it doesn't work as promised, you pay nothing. It's all our risk"

"Proprietary methodology"

"A step-by-step guide tested in 500+ companies that reduces the sales cycle by 40%"

✏️ Exercise: translate your 5 main characteristics

Now it's your turn. This exercise is the most important of the module.List 5 characteristics of your product or serviceand translate each one following the model: Feature → Benefit → Impact.

Exercise template

FEATURE:

What does your product HAVE (e.g. "real-time dashboard")

BENEFIT:

What does this DO (e.g. "show updated data automatically")

IMPACT:

What does this CHANGE (e.g. “you make decisions 5x faster and avoid losing R$X per month”)

💡 Final tip

Do this exercise andmemorize impact phrases. In the next sales conversation, use only impactful language. You will immediately notice the difference in the customer's reaction.
