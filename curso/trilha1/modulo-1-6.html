🛡️ Objections: what they are and why they exist

Understand objections as signs of interest and learn to respond with confidence and empathy.

1

🎯 What is a real objection

An objectionIt's not rejection — it's a sign of interest. Think: those who are not interested simply leave. When someone objects, they are saying: "I'm interested, but I need more confidence to decide." This shift in perspective completely transforms how you handle objections.

The difference in practice

🚪
No interest
Leave in silence. Don't ask, don't question.
❓
With interest
Objects: "It's expensive", "I need to think". You want to be convinced.

💡 Change of mindset

The next time you hear an objection, instead of feeling afraid, feelgratitude. The customer is giving you the chance to clarify the doubt that prevents the purchase.

2

🖐️ The 5 universal objections

It may seem like there are infinite objections, but in practice95% comes down to just 5 categories. Master these 5 and be prepared for practically anything the customer says.

The 5 Universal Objections

1."It's expensive"

Translated: "I didn't see enough value to justify this investment"

2."I need to think"

Translated: "I'm afraid of making the wrong decision and I need more security"

3."I'll see later"

Translated: "I didn't feel enough urgency to act now"

4."I want to compare"

Translated: "I don't understand what differentiates you from your competitors"

5."It's not a priority"

Translated: "I didn't understand the cost of not solving this now"

3

🔀 Objection vs indecision

Objection and indecision are different problemsthat require different responses. Confusing the two is a common mistake that costs sales.

Objection

  • → Specific and nameable barrier
  • → "It's expensive", "I don't have time"
  • → Solved with argument and proof
  • → Customer knows what stops them

Indecision

  • → General paralysis, without clear reason
  • → "I don't know", "Let me see"
  • → Solved with simplification and security
  • → Customer doesn’t know what’s stopping him

📊 How to differentiate

Ask:"If it weren't for [mentioned objection], would you close it?"If the answer is yes, and objection — address the specific point. If the answer is "I don't know," and it's indecision — simplify the decision and reduce the risk.

4

😰 The fear behind “it’s expensive”

"It's expensive" is almost never about money.Most of the time, it's a shield for real fear: fear of making mistakes, of not working, of regret, of being judged for the decision. When you stop lowering prices and start addressing fear, conversion goes up dramatically.

What "it's expensive" really means

"What if it doesn't work?"→ Deal with guarantees and success stories

"What if I regret it?"→ Offer a trial period or money-back guarantee

"I'm not sure if I need it"→ Go back to the diagnosis and amplify the pain

"I didn't understand the value"→ Rebuild the value stack with specific examples

💡 The right answer

Never respond “it’s expensive” with a discount. Respond with:"I understand. Tell me: what specifically concerns you about this investment?"— that reveals real fear.

5

🔍 Why objections appear

Objections havethree root causes. Identifying which one is at stake allows you to give the right answer instead of a generic answer.

The 3 Root Causes

1

Insufficient value

The customer did not see enough reason. Solution: rebuild value, show more results, use cases.

2

Fear of making mistakes

The customer is afraid of making the wrong decision. Solution: guarantees, testimonials, risk reduction.

3

Lack of urgency

The customer does not feel they need to act now. Solution: amplify the cost of inaction with implication questions.

6

🤝 The right posture when faced with an objection

O AER protocol(Welcome, Understand, Respond) is the most effective way to deal with any objection. Never argue, never invalidate, never pressure.

AER Protocol

A — Welcome

"I understand completely. It makes perfect sense to think about it before deciding."

E — Understand

"Tell me more: when you say it's expensive, and compared to what exactly?"

A — Reply

"I understand. Look, considering that the problem costs you R$X per month and our solution solves it, the investment pays for itself in [deadline]."

💡 Exercise

Write the AER response to each of the 5 universal objections to your product.Decorate and practice.At the next real objection, you will have the answer ready and confident.
