TOPIC 1

1. Real distribution in cold public

Understanding how levels of consciousness are distributed when no one knows you

When you advertise to people who have never heard of you, your company or your product, you are talking tocold public. The overwhelming majority of these people do not know they have a problem, much less that there is a solution. This completely changes the way you need to communicate.

The typical distribution of consciousness levels in cold audiences follows a very predictable pattern. Understanding these numbers is the basis for any acquisition strategy that works over the long term.

Distribution cards
Unconscious 40-60%

You don't know you have a problem. Live on "automatic pilot". No research, no search for a solution.

Aware of the problem 20-30%

You feel the pain but don't know what to do. Search for symptoms, not solutions.

Aware of the solution 10-20%

You know there are solutions, but you haven't chosen one yet. Compare options.

Product aware 5-10%

You know your product, but haven't decided yet. Need proof, assurance or encouragement.

Very aware 1-5%

Ready to buy. It just needs a final push — offer, urgency or facilitation.

💡 Practical implications

If you make an ad to a cold audience saying “Buy now with 20% off”, you are only talking to 1-5% of the audience. The other 95% don't even understand why they would need your product. You are literally throwing money away on 95% of the range.

TOPIC 2
🔥

2. Distribution in warm public

How numbers change when people already know you

A warm audience are people who have already had some contact with you: they follow you on Instagram, have visited your website, watched a video, liked a post. They're not weird, but they're not ready to buy either. The critical point here is thatthe distribution of consciousness changes drastically.

This audience has already passed the first filter. Something in its content caught attention and made the person stop. This means that the share of "unconscious" decreases significantly, and the intermediate ranges grow.

3. Hot public distribution

When the lead has already demonstrated real intention to purchase

Hot audiences include qualified leads, people who requested a quote, added to cart, participated in a webinar, downloaded specific material or got in touch. These people alreadydemonstrated intent. Communication needs to be completely different.

In the hot public, the pyramid is practically inverted. Most are already aware of the product or very aware. What is missing is trust, urgency or removal of objections.

💡 Common mistake

Treating a hot audience as cold (keeping explaining what they already know) is as harmful as treating a cold audience as hot. When someone is ready to buy and you keep "educating", you generate impatience and lose the sale.

TOPIC 4
🔄

4. Top, middle and bottom of the funnel

Where each level of consciousness fits into the traditional funnel

The traditional sales funnel (top, middle and bottom) is a simplified model that most professionals are familiar with. But few knowhow it connects directly with levels of consciousness. When you understand this correspondence, the funnel stops being a theory and becomes a practical tool.

Visual correspondence box

TOP OF THE FUNNEL (ToFu)

Attract and awaken

🔍
Unconscious Aware of the problem

Content: educational posts, curiosities, provocations, stories, memes, short videos. Objective: to draw attention and plant the seeds of the problem.

FUNNEL HALF (MoFu)

Educate and connect

📚
Aware of the solution

Content: tutorials, comparisons, webinars, ebooks, case studies. Objective: to show that there is a solution and that you are the best option.

FUNNEL FUND (BoFu)

Convert and close

💰
Product aware Very aware

Content: testimonials, demonstrations, offers, guarantees, urgency. Objective: remove the last objection and convert.

💡 Key Insight

The funnel is not a ladder — it is a filter. Out of 1,000 people at the top, maybe 100 make it to the middle and 10 to the bottom. That's why you need much more top content than bottom content. Most do the opposite and then complain that "marketing doesn't work".

TOPIC 5
⚖️

5. The 60/40 rule

Why do most people make mistakes by investing only at the bottom of the funnel?

The rule is simple:60-80% of your content and communication should be education (top and middle of the funnel). Only 20-40% should be direct offering (bottom of the funnel). This balance is not arbitrary — it reflects the actual distribution of consciousness among your audience.

Most salespeople and companies do exactly the opposite: 80% offering, 20% education. The result? Tired audience, low engagement, increasingly high acquisition costs and the feeling that "the market is saturated".

❌ What most people do

Education (ToFu/MoFu)20%
Offer (BoFu)80%

Result: angry audience, blockages, high cost, low conversion.

✅ What works

Education (ToFu/MoFu)60-80%
Offer (BoFu)20-40%

Result: trust, authority, engaged audience, natural conversion.

Why do the majority make mistakes?

  • ▸Pressure for immediate results— The manager wants to sell today, so he just makes an offer. This burns out the public quickly.
  • ▸Don't understand the funnel— You think everyone who sees the ad is ready to buy. Ignore the 95% that aren't there.
  • ▸Confuses activity with result— Sends 10 promotions a week and thinks he's "working hard." In fact, it is destroying the relationship.
  • ▸Does not measure the complete cycle— It only measures the conversion of the last click, not the cumulative effect of education at the top.

🏋️ Exercise

Analyze the last 20 communications that you or your company sent (posts, emails, messages). Classify each one as “education” or “offer”. What was the proportion? If it was more than 50% offer, you are burning your audience.

TOPIC 6
🎯

6. How to generate content for all levels simultaneously

One theme, five different approaches for five levels of consciousness

The secret is not to create 5 separate campaigns. And catcha single theme and breaks it down into 5 approaches, one for each level of consciousness. This way you cover the entire funnel with efficiency and consistency.

This strategy is what separates the amateur professional from the strategist. The amateur creates random content. The strategist creates systematic content that feeds each stage of the funnel at the same time.

Practical example with a theme

Example: Topic "Sales team productivity"

N1

Unconscious

"Did you know that most salespeople waste 3 hours a day on tasks that don't generate sales?" — Post that generates curiosity without talking about a solution

N2

Aware of the problem

"5 signs that your sales team is producing less than they should" — Content that validates the pain and names the problem

N3

Aware of the solution

"3 methods to double the productivity of your sales team" — Comparative tutorial showing options, including yours

N4

Product aware

"How company X increased productivity by 47% using our method" — Case study with concrete results

N5

Very aware

"Last 5 vacancies with 30% discount for implementation this month" — Direct offer with urgency and incentive

Choose a central theme for your business and create 5 content titles — one for each level of awareness. Use the template above as a guide. Then, organize it on a weekly calendar. You've just created an entire week's worth of strategic content in 15 minutes.
