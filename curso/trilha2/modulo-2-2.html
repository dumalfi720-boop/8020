MODULE 2.2

❓ The 4 questions that change everything

Four strategic questions that reveal the pain, quantify the problem and get the customer ready to buy.

5
Topics
~50
Minutes
Practical
Level
Technique
Type
Topic 1
1

😣 "What bothers you most about this today?"

This is the question ofdiagnostic opening. It's not "how can I help?" which is too generic. It's a question that forces the client to reflect on what hurts most at that specific moment.

Why this question works

  • ✓ And it specifies:"most bothersome" forces a choice, not a list
  • ✓ And emotional:"bothers" connects with feeling, not with logic
  • ✓ And present:"today" anchors in the current moment, not in theory
  • ✓ And open:cannot be answered with yes or no

Variations of Question 1

"If you could solve just one thing in this area, what would it be?"

“What frustrates you most about the way this works today?”

"What's the biggest bottleneck you face in this?"

💡 Practical Exercise

In the next 5 conversations (personal or professional), use this question and observe the reaction. Write down what people respond to. You will realize that almost everyone has a pain ready to share — they just need someone to ask.

Topic 2
2

📊 "How much does this cost in time, money or wear and tear?"

The second questionturns pain into a number. An abstract complaint becomes a concrete cost. "I'm dissatisfied" becomes "I lose R$10,000 per month". The difference in impact is brutal.

The 3 types of cost

⏰
Time
"How many hours a week do you spend dealing with this?"
💰
Money
“How much does this represent in lost revenue per month?”
😤
Wear
“How much stress does this create for you and your team?”

✓ With quantification

  • "I waste 10 hours/week on this process"
  • "I stop earning R$8k/month"
  • "I've already lost 3 employees because of this"

→ Creates real urgency to resolve

✗ No quantification

  • "It is complicated"
  • "It doesn't work well"
  • "The team complains"

→ Easy to ignore and snooze

📊 Perspective

When the client puts a number on pain, they create their ownreference anchor. Now any solution that costs less than that number seems like a good deal. You don't need to convince — mathematics convinces.

Topic 3
3

⏳ "What happens if nothing changes?"

The third questionprojects the negative future. It makes the client imagine life in 6, 12 months without solving the problem. And it activates the most powerful principle in decision psychology: loss aversion.

The projection of the negative future

📍
Today
Present problem
← No action →
🔥
6 months
Worse problem
←Continuing →
💀
12 months
Real crisis

Example in practice

Seller:"You said you lose R$15,000 a month with bad days. If nothing changes in the next 12 months, how much does that represent?"

Customer:"R$ 180 thousand... Wow, I had never thought of it like that."

Seller:"And beyond the financial, how does this affect your motivation? Your team?"

💡 Important care

This question is not meant to scare or manipulate. And to help the client see what he already knows but hasn't stopped to calculate.The tone should be that of a partner, not a threat.

Topic 4
4

✨ "What would be a good solution for you?"

The fourth question is the most powerful becausereverse the dynamics. Instead of you presenting the solution, the client describes what he considers ideal. If your product fits what he just described, the sale practically closes itself.

The mechanics of self-convincing

1

Client describes the ideal

"I would need something that would bring customers during the week, without me having to post every day on social media"

2

You confirm the fit

"That's exactly what we do. We create automated campaigns that attract customers on slow days without you having to use social media."

✓

Customer convinced himself

He doesn't feel like you sold out—he feels like he found what he needed.

📊 Why is this so effective?

Basic psychology:People trust their own conclusions more than those of others. When the client designs the solution and you show that you have exactly that, they feel like they made the decision alone. There is no objection to the idea itself.

Topic 5
5

🔗 How to chain the 4 questions in a natural conversation

The 4 questions are not an interrogation. They arenatural transitions within a conversation. Between each question, you listen, validate, comment and only then ask the next one. Secret and naturalness.

The natural flow of the 4 questions

QUESTION 1 → PAIN

"What bothers you most about this today?"

→ Listen, validate: "I understand, it makes sense..."

QUESTION 2 → COST

"And how much does this cost you? In time, money, wear and tear?"

→ Repeat the number: "So it's R$X per month..."

QUESTION 3 → CONSEQUENCE

"And if it continues like this, what happens?"

→ Help calculate: "In 12 months, this turns into R$ Y..."

QUESTION 4 → IDEAL SOLUTION

"What would be a good solution for you?"

→ Connect: “Look, that’s exactly what we do…”

✓ Natural transitions

  • "It makes sense. And tell me..."
  • "I see. Let me ask you..."
  • "Interesting. And you already calculated..."
  • "Cool. So in your view..."

✗ Robotic transitions

  • "Now for question number 2..."
  • "Moving on to the next point..."
  • "Following my script..."
  • "The next question is..."

💡 Final Exercise

Record yourself asking the 4 questions with a friend simulating the client. Watch the recording and evaluate:Does it look like a conversation or an interrogation?Adjust until it looks natural. This may take 5-10 attempts — and is normal.
