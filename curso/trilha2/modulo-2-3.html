👋 Opening: how to start without sounding salesy

The first 30 seconds define everything. If you sound like a salesman, the customer raises his defenses. The secret andstart as a consultant, not as a salesperson. Show genuine interest before anything else.

Template opening script

PRESENTIAL / VIDEO

"Hi [name], nice! Before anything else, I want to understand your situation better. I don't want to offer you anything that doesn't make sense to you. Can I ask you a few questions?"

WHATSAPP / MESSAGE

"Hi [name]! I saw that you were interested in [product/service]. Before I give you any information, tell me: what motivated you to look for this now?"

PHONE / COLD CALL

"Hi [name], this is [your name] from [company]. I'm calling you because I work in [area] and have helped companies like yours with [result]. Do you have 2 minutes? I want to ask you a quick question."

✓ Openings that work

  • Ask permission to ask
  • Show genuine interest
  • Talk about results, not products
  • Be direct about the reason for contact

✗ Openings that separate

  • "I have an incredible opportunity!"
  • "Can I make you an offer?"
  • "Do you know our product?"
  • "Let me show you what we do"

💡 Golden Rule of Opening

In the first 30 seconds,speak 20% and listen 80%. Your only mission in opening is to get permission to ask questions. Nothing more.

🔍 Development: how to conduct diagnosis

With the client's permission, you enter the diagnosis phase. Here you use the4 questions from Module 2.2to map the pain, quantify the problem and project consequences.

Step-by-step diagnostic script

STEP 1: CURRENT SITUATION

"Tell me, how does [relevant area] work for you today? What is the current process?"

→ Listen carefully. Make a mental note of the pain points.

STEP 2: MAIN PAIN

"And in this process, what bothers you most? What would you change first if you could?"

→ Validate: "It makes perfect sense. Other customers of ours had the same problem."

STEP 3: QUANTIFICATION

"And if we put it on the tip of the pencil, how much do you think it costs you per month? In time, money or wear and tear?"

→ Repeat the number: "So we're talking about R$X per month..."

STEP 4: CONSEQUENCE

"And if it continues like this for the next 6-12 months, what happens?"

→ Help calculate: "In 12 months, that would be R$ Y..."

📊 Well done diagnosis checklist

  • ✓Do you know the customer's main pain?
  • ✓The customer verbalized the cost of the problem
  • ✓The client saw the consequence of not resolving
  • ✓You spoke less than 30% of the total time

🔄 Transition: from problem to solution

The transition is the most delicate moment. Do you needexit listener mode and enter presenter modewithout the client feeling that everything was a trap to get to the pitch.

3 ways to make the transition

Transition by summary

"So, in short: you have [problem

Transition by permission

"Based on everything you've told me, I think I can help. Do you want me to show you how?"

Transition by story

"Interesting, because I had a client in the same situation — [name/sector] — and he got [result]. Do you want to know what he did?"

💡 The summary rule

Before presenting any solution,summarize what the customer said and ask for confirmation. When he confirms, you have the "license" to present. And he feels like you really listened.

Topic 4
4

🎁 Presentation: show value without listing features

Don't list features.Connect each benefit to a pain the customer mentioned. Use his words. Make him feel that the solution was tailor-made.

The formula Pain → Solution → Result

PAIN (client's words)

"You told me you waste 2 hours a day on manual reports..."

SOLUTION (your product)

"What we do is automate these reports. Instead of 2 hours, it takes 5 minutes."

RESULT (what changes)

“This gives you back almost 10 hours a week to focus on what really generates revenue.”

✓ Presentation with value

  • Use the customer's words
  • Connects feature to specific pain
  • Shows tangible results
  • Talk about a maximum of 3 key benefits

✗ Valueless presentation

  • List 10+ features
  • Use technical jargon
  • Talk about the product, not the customer
  • Show generic slides

📊 Rule of 3

Never present more than3 main benefits. The human brain retains 3 things easily. If you list 10, the customer won't remember any of them. Choose the 3 most connected with the pain he mentioned.

Topic 5
5

🤝 Closing: drive the decision without pressure

Closing is not a pressure technique. And thenatural conclusion of a well-conducted conversation. If you did the diagnosis well, the sale is already 80% closed before you order.

Closing scripts

Closing by confirmation

"Based on everything we've talked about, does it make sense for you to go through with this?"

Closing next step

"What would be the best next step? Can I prepare the proposal for tomorrow?"

Closing by alternative

"Would you prefer to start with the basic plan or the full plan?"

Closing due to natural urgency

"You said you lose R$5k a month with this. Each month that passes adds another R$5k. When would you like to start solving it?"

💡 After asking for the sale

Ask the closing question andstay silent. Don't fill the silence with justifications. Silence creates space for the client to think and decide. Whoever speaks first after closing loses.

📋 Complete template script

Here is the complete script bringing together all the phases.Copy, adapt to your product and start using. You don't have to be perfect the first time — improve with each conversation.

Complete script — Model 8020

PHASE 1 — OPENING (30 seconds)

"Hi [name], nice! Before anything else, I want to understand your situation. I don't want to offer you anything that doesn't make sense. Can I ask you a few quick questions?"

PHASE 2 — DIAGNOSIS (5-10 minutes)

"Tell me, how does [area] work for you today?"
[To hear]
"And what bothers you most about this?"
[Listen, validate]
"How much do you think this costs you per month?"
[Let it calculate]
"What if it continues like this for another 12 months?"

PHASE 3 — TRANSITION (30 seconds)

"So, in short: you have [problem], it costs you [amount], and in 12 months it would be [amount x12]. Correct? ... Can I show you how other customers solved this?"

PHASE 4 — PRESENTATION (3-5 minutes)

"You said [pain 1] — we can solve this with [benefit 1]."
"You mentioned that [pain 2] — our [feature] eliminates that."
“And about [pain 3] — our clients resolved this within [deadline].”

PHASE 5 — PRICE AND CLOSING (1-2 minutes)

"The investment is R$ [value]. Considering that you lose R$ [cost of pain], the return is paid in [term]. Does it make sense for you to start?"
[Silence. Wait for response.]

📊 How to adapt this script

  • ✓Replace [area], [problem], [benefit] with your context
  • ✓Practice out loud until it sounds natural
  • ✓Record and review — adjust what feels robotic
  • ✓Use 5-10 times before judging whether it works
