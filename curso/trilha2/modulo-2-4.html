🛡️ How to answer the 5 most common objections

Ready and strategic responses to the objections that appear in 90% of sales. Never be without knowing what to say again.

Topic 1
1

💸 "It's expensive"

"It's expensive" rarely means the price is too high. Most often it means"I didn't see enough value". The answer is not to discount — and to rebuild the value.

3 ready responses to "It's expensive"

Answer 1: Compare with the cost of pain

"I understand. Tell me: you said you lose R$15 thousand per month with this problem. The investment is R$2 thousand. If we solve just 20% of the problem, it will already pay for itself. Do you think it's worth it?"

Answer 2: Asking "expensive compared to what?"

"Expensive compared to what? What do you lose today by not solving it? What does a competitor charge? Help me understand what would be a fair value for you."

Answer 3: Split the daily cost

"R$2,000 per month seems like a lot, but it's R$66 per day. It's less than lunch. To solve a problem that costs you R$500 per day, would you invest R$66?"

💡 Never do this

Never offer a discount immediatelywhen you hear "it's expensive". First, rebuild the value. If, after rebuilding the value, the customer still finds it expensive, then yes, you can negotiate — but from a position of strength.

Topic 2
2

🤔 "I need to think"

"I need to think" usually means"I have a doubt that I'm not talking about"or "I'm not convinced enough." The key is to help the client think out loud — right there, with you.

Strategic responses

Validate and deepen

"Of course, it makes sense to want to think. Tell me: what specifically do you need to evaluate? Maybe I can help with some missing information."

Identify the real doubt

"Totally fair. Normally when someone needs to think, it's because they have a doubt about [price / if it works / timing]. What's yours?"

Ensure follow-up

"No problem. When would be a good time for me to talk to you again? Tuesday or Wednesday?"

📊 Important Statistics

90% of clients who say “I need to think” and don’t have a follow-up scheduled never come back.Always leave with a set date and time to resume the conversation.

Topic 3
3

📅 "I'll see later"

"I'll see later" is the softer version of "I don't want to decide now". The strategy andTurn the vague “later” into a specific commitmentand show the cost of each day of waiting.

How to keep the lead warm

Create natural urgency

"I understand. Just one thing: you mentioned that you lose R$500 a day with this. Each day that passes adds another R$500. When would be the ideal 'later' for you?"

Offer something of value

"No problem. Can I send you material with [relevant content] in the meantime? And on [day of the week], can I send you a message to see what you think?"

Schedule the next contact

"Deal. Can I call you on Thursday at 2pm to continue this conversation? I'll write it down here in the diary."

💡 Rule of Commitment

Never end a conversation with "later" without havingdefined day, time and channelfor the next contact. "After" without a date is the same as "never".

Topic 4
4

⚖️ "I want to compare"

When the customer wants to compare,the worst thing you can do is talk bad about your competitor. The best thing is to help the client define the right comparison criteria — criteria where you naturally stand out.

The criteria technique

Step 1:"It makes perfect sense to compare. Tell me: what is most important to you in this decision? Price? Support? Result? Speed?"

Step 2:[Client answers the criteria]

Step 3:"Great. Using these criteria you mentioned, can I show you how we position ourselves?" [Show your strengths exactly in these criteria]

✓ How to position yourself

  • Help define criteria
  • Show your differences
  • Use evidence and testimonials
  • Respect the competitor

✗ What not to do

  • Talking bad about your competitor
  • Lower price out of fear
  • Press to decide now
  • Ignore the need to compare

📊 The truth about comparison

Whoever defines the comparison criteria wins the sale.If the customer compares by price, whoever is cheaper wins. If you compare results, whoever delivers the most wins. Help the client compare based on where you are stronger.

Topic 5
5

🔥 "It's not a priority now"

"Not a priority" means that the customerdoes not feel enough urgency. Either the diagnosis was not deep enough, or the consequence was not clear. The answer is to reactivate the pain with respect.

How to reactivate the pain

Reconnect with the pain

"I understand. Tell me: that problem you mentioned — losing R$5,000 a month — are you comfortable with continuing to lose that while taking care of other priorities?"

Show the cost of waiting

"Of course, priorities change. Just a reflection: if you wait 3 months, that's another R$15,000 lost. Is there anything that would need to change for this to become a priority?"

Sowing for the future

"No problem. Can I send you content that could help when the time comes? And when would be a good time to resume this conversation?"

When "not a priority" is real

Sometimes, it really isn't a priority. And that's okay. In these cases, your mission is:

  • ✓Keep the door open respectfully
  • ✓Schedule a follow-up in 30-60 days
  • ✓Send valuable content periodically
  • ✓Be reminded when priority changes

💡 The right mindset

Objections are not rejections — they arerequests for more information. When the customer objects, he is saying "convince me better", not "go away". Treat each objection as an opportunity to deepen the conversation.
