📅 The 7-day training

A complete, day-by-day program to transform theory into practical skills. Each day focuses on a specific skill with clear and progressive exercises.

How to use this program

This is a practical 7-day program. Each day has a unique focus and specific exercises. Don't skip days — the sequence is progressive. Set aside at least 45-60 minutes a day. If possible, do it with a training partner.

DAY 1
📝

Day 1: Learn the right questions

DAY 1

Total focus on the 4 fundamental questions

The first day is dedicated exclusively to questions. Don't sell anything. Do not present the product. Don't talk about price. Today you areask and listen. It seems simple, but it is the most difficult exercise for most salespeople because it goes against the instinct to "convince".

The 4 fundamental questions you will master today:

P1

Status question

“How does [process X] work in your company today?”

Objective: understand the current context without assuming anything.

P2

Problem question

"What's the biggest difficulty you face with this?"

Objective: bring the pain to the surface.

P3

Impact question

“How much does this problem cost in time/money/energy per month?”

Objective: quantify the cost of not resolving.

P4

Wish question

"If you could change one thing in this area, what would it be?"

Objective: reveal the desired future state.

🏋️ Day 1 Exercise

  • 1.Write the 4 questions adapted to YOUR product/service.
  • 2.Practice in pairs: one asks, the other answers like a real customer. 10 minutes each.
  • 3.Repeat 3 times, each time with a different "customer" (different segments).
  • 4.Rule: you CANNOT present a product. If you feel like it, stop and ask another question.
DAY 2
😣

Day 2: Identify pain and desire

DAY 2

Listen to answers and extract what really matters

Yesterday you learned to ask. Today you will learn toreally listen to the answers. Most salespeople hear the words, but don't identify what's behind them. The client says "I need something faster" — the pain is not "speed", and thefrustration with the delay and the impact it causes.

Today you will practice mappingcurrent state vs future state. This map is the most powerful sales weapon because it shows the distance between where the customer IS and where they WANT to be.

Visual map

Map: Current State vs Future State

CURRENT STATUS
  • ▸ What is the situation today?
  • ▸ What causes frustration?
  • ▸ How much does it cost (time/money/energy)?
  • ▸ How long have you been living with this?
  • ▸ What have you tried to do?
FUTURE STATE
  • ▸ What would the ideal scenario look like?
  • ▸ What would change in your routine?
  • ▸ How much would you save?
  • ▸ How would you feel?
  • ▸ What could you do that you can’t do today?

🏋️ Day 2 Exercise

  • 1.Do the 4 questions from Day 1 with a partner (as a review).
  • 2.After each answer, identify: which is the PAIN and which is the DESIRE behind it.
  • 3.Fill in the Current State vs Future State map with the “client’s” responses.
  • 4.Confirm with the client: "If I understand correctly, today you are [current state] and you would like to be [future state]. And that's it?"
DAY 3
💎

Day 3: Transform characteristics into value

DAY 3

From features to real impact on customer lives

Feature and what the product DOES. Value and what it CHANGES in the customer’s life. The customer does not buy characteristics — they buy transformation. Today you will train the ability totranslate any feature into concrete impact.

The formula is simple: take the characteristic and complete it with one of these phrases —"this reduces...", "this speeds up...", "this prevents...", "this increases...". If you can't complete it, the feature is probably not relevant to that customer.

Translation examples: Feature → Value

FEATURE

"Our system has automatic reports"

VALUE

"Thatreducesfrom 4 hours to 15 minutes the time you spend putting together reports every week"

FEATURE

"We have 24/7 support"

VALUE

"ThatavoidMay you lose sales over the weekend because the system crashed and no one responded"

FEATURE

"Integration with WhatsApp"

VALUE

"Thatacceleratelead response time from hours to seconds, before it goes to the competitor"

🏋️ Day 3 Exercise

  • 1.List 5 characteristics of your product or service.
  • 2.For each, write: "This reduces/accelerates/avoids/increases [concrete impact]."
  • 3.Practice speaking out loud. Time it: each value phrase must be a maximum of 15 seconds.
  • 4.Ask your partner to evaluate: "Did this convince me? Why yes/no?"
DAY 4
📋

Day 4: Follow the full script

DAY 4

Practice the value roadmap from start to finish

In the first 3 days, you trained each piece separately: questions, listening, value translation. Today is the day ofput everything together in a continuous flow. You will follow the complete itinerary from start to finish, without skipping steps.

The key and therepetition. Do it at least 3-5 times. At first, it will seem mechanical and forced. In the third, it starts to flow. On Thursday, it already seems natural. This is normal — and exactly how it works.

Complete service itinerary

1

Opening (1-2 min)

Presentation + rapport + defining the purpose of the conversation

2

Investigation (5-8 min)

4 fundamental questions + active listening + pain/desire mapping

3

Confirmation (1 min)

"If I understand correctly..." — confirm pain and desire before presenting

4

Value presentation (3-5 min)

Connect each feature with the customer’s specific pain/desire

5

Verification (1 min)

"Does it make sense to you?" — check receptivity before closing

6

Next step (1-2 min)

Propose concrete action: schedule demo, send proposal, close

🏋️ Day 4 Exercise

  • 1.Do the complete itinerary with a partner. Time it: It should last 12-18 minutes.
  • 2.The partner scores 1-5 for each step using the checklist from the previous module.
  • 3.Repeat at least 3 times. Each round, focus on improving your weakest point.
  • 4.In the last round, record audio for later review.
DAY 5
🛡️

Day 5: Respond to objections

DAY 5

Train the 5 most common objections until they become natural

Objections are not rejections — they are requests for information. When the customer says "it's expensive", he is saying"convince me that it's worth the investment". When you say "I need to think", you are saying"I'm not sure enough yet".

The AER (Welcome, Explore, Respond) protocol is the framework for handling any objection without appearing defensive or desperate.

AER Protocol — The 5 objections with ready answers

1. "It's expensive"

A — Welcome: "I see, investment is something that needs to make sense."

E — Explore: "Expensive compared to what? Or is it a question of available budget?"

A — Reply: "You mentioned that you lose R$X per month with [problem]. In Y months, the investment pays off and starts generating savings."

2. "I need to think"

A — Welcome: "Of course, it's an important decision."

E — Explore: "So I can help you think: what specifically do you want to evaluate better?"

A — Reply: Answer the specific question and propose a deadline: "Does it make sense for us to talk again on Wednesday?"

3. "I already have a supplier"

A — Welcome: "Great, it's good to have someone you trust."

E — Explore: “If you could improve one thing about your current service, what would it be?”

A — Reply: Focus exactly on the gap that the current supplier leaves.

4. "It's not the time"

A — Welcome: "I see, timing is important."

E — Explore: “What would need to happen for it to be the right time?”

A — Reply: "In the meantime, how much do you continue to lose per month with [problem]? Sometimes the best time is before the cost accumulates."

5. "I'm going to see my partner/wife"

A — Welcome: "It makes perfect sense to decide together."

E — Explore: "For you personally, does it make sense? What would be [partner/wife]'s main concern?"

A — Reply: "Can I prepare a summary with the main points to facilitate the conversation? Or can we have a quick call with him/her?"

🏋️ Day 5 Exercise

  • 1.Partner writes the complete script and throws ONE objection. You respond with AER.
  • 2.Repeat with each of the 5 objections. Don't announce which one is coming — it should be a surprise.
  • 3.Evaluate: was the response natural or did it seem memorized? If it seems memorized, repeat.
  • 4.Objective: answer any of the 5 objections in less than 30 seconds, naturally.
DAY 6
🎥

Day 6: Recorded simulations

DAY 6

Simulate, record, watch and correct in 2-3 rounds

Today is the most intense day of the program. You will docomplete service simulations, recording everything, and then watch together with the partner to identify areas for improvement. Is it uncomfortable? Yes. And the most effective method? Undoubtedly.

The goal is to do 2-3 full rounds, each one better than the last. Firstly, you identify the problems. In the second, try to correct it. In the third, it consolidates the improvements.

Session structure (60 min total)

Round 1 (20 min) Recording + Review

10 minutes of recorded simulation + 10 minutes watching together and taking notes. Identify the 3 biggest points for improvement.

Round 2 (20 min) Correction + Review

10 min simulation focusing on the 3 points. Record again. 10 min review comparing to the first round.

Round 3 (20 min) Consolidation + Assessment

10 minutes of final simulation — the "best version". 10 min final assessment: grade for each step of the checklist.

What to note during the review

▸Moments when you got lost or didn't know what to say
▸Questions you forgot to ask
▸Transitions that became abrupt
▸Times when you talked too much without listening
▸Body language (if recorded video)
▸Moments when the "customer" was most engaged

💡 Important tip

Don't judge yourself harshly in the first round. Everyone feels awkward seeing each other for the first time. Discomfort is temporary — learning is permanent. By the second round, you will be 30-50% better.

DAY 7
🚀

Day 7: Real care with supervision

DAY 7

The first real service applying everything you have trained

The day has come. After 6 days of preparation, today you will doreal serviceusing everything you trained. The difference is that you don't go alone — a manager, experienced colleague or training partner will observe (live or listening to the call) and give immediate feedback after the service.

This is not "proof". And the bridge between training and reality. The goal is not perfection — andconscious applicationthan he was trained.

Day 7 Protocol

A

Before service (10 min)

  • ▸ Review the script mentally
  • ▸ Remember the 4 questions and the AER protocol
  • ▸ Define with the observer: he takes notes, does not interfere
  • ▸ Breathe. You trained. You are ready.
B

During service

  • ▸ Follow the script, but don’t be afraid to adapt
  • ▸ If you get stuck, ask a question — asking always works
  • ▸ Perfection is not covered; focus on executing each step
  • ▸ The observer remains silent, just taking notes
C

After service (15 min)

  • ▸ Immediate feedback — maximum 15 min later, while still fresh
  • ▸ Salesperson speaks first: "What did I do well? What would I do differently?"
  • ▸ Observer complements using OIS format
  • ▸ Set 2-3 points for the next service

What to expect from Day 7

It will work if...

  • ▸ You followed the script for the most part
  • ▸ Asked at least 3 of the 4 questions
  • ▸ Listened more than spoke
  • ▸ Proposed a clear next step

It's normal if...

  • ▸ Forgot a step
  • ▸ He was nervous at first
  • ▸ The objection took me by surprise
  • ▸ Did not close the sale

🎯 The real goal

Day 7 is not about closing a sale. And about proving to yourself thatCan you follow a method in a real situation?. If you did this, the training was a success. The sale will come as a consequence of repetition.
