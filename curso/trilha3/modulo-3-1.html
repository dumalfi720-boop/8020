🧬 The 4 factors of the business profile

Understand the 4 factors that determine the commercial potential of any professional and how to combine them into an objective evaluation formula.

Topic 1

🗣️ Natural fitness

Natural fitness andinnate ease in communicating, persuasion and reading people. And the factor that is most immediately noticeable: the person who enters a room is naturally captivated, who perceives non-verbal signals, who adapts the tone of voice and speech instinctively.

Main Box

The 5 components of natural fitness

  • ✓ Fluid communication— Articulates ideas clearly, without visible effort
  • ✓ natural persuasion— Takes conversations where you want them to go without seeming manipulative
  • ✓ Reading people—Notices mood, hidden objections, and buying signals
  • ✓ Speech adaptation— Changes the approach depending on the interlocutor’s profile
  • ✓ Charisma— Builds connection and trust quickly
Comparison Grid

✓ High Aptitude

  • "Talk to anyone naturally"
  • "Notice when the customer is hesitant"
  • "Adapts tone in real time"
  • "Builds trust in minutes"

✗ Low Fitness

  • "Follows a rigid script, without adapting"
  • "Doesn't understand non-verbal signals"
  • "Use the same tone with everyone"
  • "It takes time to create rapport"
Practical Tip

💡 Important

Natural fitnessis not decisive alone. A salesperson with low aptitude but a high desire and ability to learn can outperform a salesperson with high aptitude who does not try hard. Aptitude is a starting point, not a destination.

Topic 2
2

🔥 Willingness to sell

Willingness to sell and thegenuine desire to be in the trading game. It's not just about "accepting" the role - it's about enjoying the challenge, putting up with the "no's", being hungry to reach goals and feeling pleasure when closing a deal. It's the fuel that keeps the seller moving even on bad days.

Detail Box

Signs of high willingness to sell

1

Proactivity

Look for leads on your own, don’t wait for the company to bring them. Call, send a message, prospect.

2

Resilience

Hears 10 "no" and calls the 11th with the same energy. Don't get rejected by people.

3

Goal hunger

Look at the goal not as pressure, but as a game. You want to beat, you want to overcome, you want to be the best.

4

Pleasure in closing

Feel real satisfaction when converting. The customer's "yes" and genuine emotional reward.

Insight

📊 Perspective

Will is the factor that mostdetermines the permanencefrom a seller. Salespeople with high aptitude but no desire leave the area within months. Salespeople with high will persist, learn and eventually perform.

📈 Ability to learn

The ability to learn - orcoachability- and the ability to receive feedback, process, adjust the speech and evolve quickly. It is the most underestimated factor by managers and, paradoxically, the most determining factor in the medium term.

Concept Box

The commercial learning cycle

📋
Receive feedback
🧠
Process and understand
🔄
Adjust your practice
📈
The result evolves
Comparison

✓ High ability to learn

  • → Proactively ask for feedback
  • → Adjust speech on the next call
  • → Study on your own
  • → Accepts criticism without defensiveness

Low ability to learn

  • → Repeat the same mistakes
  • → Justify instead of adjust
  • → Just do what is told
  • → Gets defensive with feedback
Tip

💡 How to test in practice

Give specific feedback after a call or meeting. Observe whether in the next interaction the sellerTried to apply what you suggested. You don't need to get it right - you need to try. That's coachability.

📊 The truth bothers

Topic 5
5

🧮 The potential formula

The potential formula combines the 4 factors withweights that reflect their relative importance. Aptitude has the greatest weight because it is the initial accelerator, but will, learning and execution together overcome aptitude - exactly as it happens in practice.

Main Formula

The Formula

P = (A x 0.35) + (V x 0.25) + (L x 0.20) + (E x 0.20)

Where: A = Aptitude | V = Will | L = Learning | E = Execution

35%
Fitness

20%

20%

Practical Example

Practical example: 2 salespeople

Carlos (Natural talent)

A=23, V=15, L=12, E=11

P = (23x0.35)+(15x0.25)+(12x0.20)+(11x0.20)

P = 8.05 + 3.75 + 2.40 + 2.20 = 16.40

Ana (Effort and method)

A=14, V=23, L=22, E=21

P = (14x0.35)+(23x0.25)+(22x0.20)+(21x0.20)

P = 4.90 + 5.75 + 4.40 + 4.20 = 19.25

Ana surpasses Carlos even with much lower aptitude. The combination of will, learning and execution compensated and surpassed natural talent.

Tip

💡 How to use the formula

The formula is not to rank sellers among themselves. And forunderstand where to invest in each person’s development. If the seller has a low score in execution but high in others, the focus is clear: discipline and routine.
