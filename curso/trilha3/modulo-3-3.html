📝 Evaluation matrix with score

Practical tool with 20 questions organized into 4 blocks to generate an objective score for each salesperson. Includes the actual questions and the grading system.

Instructions

How to use this matrix

Each question receives a grade of1 to 5. At the end of each block, add up the 5 scores to obtain the factor score (minimum 5, maximum 25). Use the scale below:

21-25
HIGH
14-20
AVERAGE
5-13
LOW
Topic 1: Aptitude Block
1

🗣️ Block 1: Natural Aptitude (5 questions)

Evaluate theThe salesperson's natural ability to communicate, persuade and read people. Observe actual behaviors, not what he says about himself. Each question is graded from 1 (very low) to 5 (excellent).

A1

Verbal communication

Does the salesperson express himself clearly and fluidly in conversations with customers? Can you explain the product/service in a simple and convincing way?

1=Confused and stuck | 3=Reasonable | 5=Fluid and clear

A2

Persuasive capacity

Can you guide the client towards closing without seeming forced? Do you use arguments that connect with the customer’s real needs?

1=Not convincing | 3=Convinces sometimes | 5=Persuades naturally

A3

Reading people

Do you notice non-verbal signals, changes in mood, unspoken objections and the customer's level of interest during the conversation?

1=Doesn't understand anything | 3=Notices obvious signs | 5=Read between the lines

A4

Speech adaptation

Adjust tone, language and approach according to the client’s profile (technical, executive, operational)? Do you speak the interlocutor's "language"?

1=Same speech for everyone | 3=Partially adjusts | 5=Natural chameleon

A5

Rapport creation

Does it generate connection and trust quickly? Does the customer feel comfortable and open in the presence of this salesperson?

1=Client is uncomfortable | 3=Neutral connection | 5=Builds trust quickly

Topic 2: Block Will
2

🔥 Block 2: Willingness to Sell (5 questions)

Evaluate theseller's genuine desire to be in sales. Observe spontaneous behaviors, not rehearsed responses. The will manifests itself in attitudes, not in words.

V1

Pleasure in the commercial game

Do you demonstrate that you enjoy the sales process? Do you talk about sales enthusiastically? Do you celebrate closings with genuine energy?

1=Seems to suffer selling | 3=Neutral | 5=Visibly in love

V2

Rejection tolerance

How do you react to a “no”? Recover quickly and move on to the next one? Or do you become paralyzed, unmotivated or complaining?

1=Collapses with rejection | 3=Uncomfortable but carry on | 5=Uses as fuel

V3

Goal hunger

Do you see the goal as an exciting challenge or as a threat? Are you looking to surpass the target or just "survive" the month?

1=Ignores or fears the goal | 3=Seeks to achieve | 5=Always wants to overcome

V4

Commercial proactiveness

Do you search for leads and opportunities on your own? Or do you just work with what the company delivers? Do you take initiative without being told to?

1=Just do what you're told | 3=Take initiative sometimes | 5=Always looking for more

V5

Self-motivation

Do you maintain energy and pace even in bad weeks? Do you need the manager to be "pulling" you or do you keep moving on your own?

1=Needs to be pushed | 3=Stay supported | 5=Self-generating energy

Topic 3: Learning Block
3

📈 Block 3: Learning Capacity (5 questions)

Evaluate thecoachability and speed of evolutionfrom the seller. This is the factor that most predicts future performance. Notice what happens AFTER the feedback, not during it.

L1

Receptivity to feedback

How do you react when you receive constructive criticism? Do you listen openly or defend yourself? Are you grateful for the feedback or resentful?

1=Becomes defensive/resentful | 3=Listen but does not always apply | 5=Give thanks and act

L2

Adjustment speed

After receiving specific feedback, how long does it take to implement the change? In the next interaction? Next week? Never?

1=Never adjusts | 3=Adjusts in days | 5=Adjusts in the next iteration

L3

Active search for knowledge

Do you study on your own? Read about sales, listen to podcasts, order materials? Or do you only consume what is mandatory?

1=No extra interest | 3=Studies when indicated | 5=Voracious self-taught

L4

Application of what you learned

Transform knowledge into practice? Does what you learn in training show up in real calls and meetings?

1=Knows but doesn't do | 3=Partially applies | 5=Implements everything you learn

L5

Visible evolution

Comparing with 30-60 days ago, is there noticeable evolution? Is today's salesperson better than yesterday's?

1=Stagnant | 3=Slow evolution | 5=Fast and consistent evolution

Topic 4: Execution Block
4

⚙️ Block 4: Consistent Execution (5 questions)

Evaluate thediscipline and consistency in the commercial routine. This block measures what the salesperson DOES, not what he KNOWS or WANTS. Use CRM data and direct observation.

E1

Compliance with the daily routine

Do the minimum number of daily activities (calls, emails, meetings)? Do you stick to your routine even on difficult days?

1=Rarely complies | 3=Complies most days | 5=Complies every day

E2

Pipeline management

Do you keep your CRM updated? Register interactions? Move deals between stages? Or is his pipeline an outdated mess?

1=Pipeline chaos | 3=Updates with delay | 5=Impeccable and real-time pipeline

E3

Systematic follow-up

Do you follow-up consistently and at the right time? Or do you let leads cool off due to forgetfulness or procrastination?

1=Does not follow-up | 3=Do it when you remember | 5=Impeccable follow-up system

E4

Preparation for meetings

Research the client before the meeting? Prepare questions? Are you clear on your objective? Or do you enter "improvised"?

1=Zero preparation | 3=Basic preparation | 5=Deep research and clear objective

E5

Delivery of results

Do the numbers reflect the effort? Does it meet the target or consistently fall short? Is the result predictable or volatile?

1=Much below target | 3=Close to goal | 5=Consistently outperforms

Topic 5: Classification
5

🏷️ Classification and interpretation of scores

With the 4 scores in hand, it's time toclassify and interpret. The classification is not a permanent label - it is a portrait of the moment that guides development actions.

Classification Table

Rating ranges by factor

Score Classification Meaning
21-25 High Featured in the factor. Natural or developed strong point.
14-20 Average Potential with room to grow. It doesn't impede it but it doesn't stand out.
5-13 Low Need for intense development or redirection.
Profile Example

Example: Full profile of a seller

22
Fitness (HIGH)
17
Willingness (MEDIA)
21
Learning (HIGH)
11
Execution (LOW)

Interpretation:Salesperson with high potential (high aptitude + learning) but who does not perform the routine. The focus of development is clear: discipline, process and close routine monitoring. Average desire indicates that you may need additional motivation.

Weighted score:(22x0.35)+(17x0.25)+(21x0.20)+(11x0.20) = 7.70+4.25+4.20+2.20 =18.35

Tip

💡 Practical Exercise

Choose 3 salespeople from your team now andapply the 20 questions. Don't look for absolute accuracy the first time - look for an honest portrayal. Practice will refine your ability to evaluate over time.
