🏷️ Groups A, B, C and D

Classify each salesperson into one of the 4 groups and define the most appropriate development strategy to maximize each person's potential.

The 4 Groups and their Strategies

Group A — Natural Talent
High aptitude + Medium/high will
Develop for performance

Topic 1: Group A
1

⭐ Group A — Natural talent

High aptitude + Medium/high will.These are the salespeople who arrive already performing. They have natural ease and want (or may want) to be in sales. The manager's role is to accelerate development and keep the challenge high to retain this talent.

Group A development plan

1

Above average goals

Don't give the same goal as the team. This seller needs a bigger challenge. Easy goal demotivates this profile.

2

Increasing autonomy

Reduce micromanagement progressively. Freedom to experiment with your own approaches.

3

Mentoring and leadership role

Ask him to mentor salespeople from Group B. This develops leadership and recognizes competence.

4

Clear career plan

Show the way: senior, coordinator, manager. This profile leaves if you don't see a future in the company.

💡 Retention alert

Group A sellers are the mostcontested by the market. If you don't offer challenge, recognition and perspective, another company will. Retaining this group is the direct responsibility of the manager.

Topic 2: Group B
2

🔨 Group B — Buildable Talent

Medium/low aptitude, high Will and Learning.This is the group where the manager generates the most impact. They are professionals who were not born ready for sales, but have an unshakable desire and learn quickly. With method and follow-up, they can become excellent salespeople.

Group B development plan

📝
Scripts and playbooks
Concrete tools to overcome the lack of naturalness. The script is the "crutch" until you gain confidence.
🎯
Progressive goals
Start with smaller goals and increase them. Each achievement fuels confidence.
👥
Intensive follow-up
Weekly 1:1 meetings, listening to calls, detailed and frequent feedback.
🔄
Weekly role play
Simulate real situations in a safe environment. Making mistakes in training to get it right in the game.

Evolution expectations

  • → 30 days: understand the process
  • → 60 days: runs with support
  • → 90 days: runs autonomously
  • → 180 days: can achieve medium-high performance

Signs that it's working

  • → Learning score remains high
  • → Execution score increases gradually
  • → Results improve month by month
  • → Trust in interactions increases

📊 Insight

Group B sellers often becomethe most loyal on the team. They know they have been developed, value the investment and repay it with dedication. Many top performers in the market came from this group.

Topic 3: Group C
3

🚧 Group C — Commercial resistance

You may have the aptitude but refuse to sell.This group demands investigation before action. Resistance can have removable causes (limiting beliefs, bad experience) or definitive causes (genuine incompatibility with the function). The role of the manager is to find out which is the case.

Investigation protocol

1

Exploratory conversation (week 1)

"What do you think about sales? What was your previous experience like? What bothers you about the role?" Listen without judging.

2

Reframing (week 2-3)

If the block is belief, show consultative selling as different from "pushing product." Of real examples of ethical and successful sellers.

3

Practice test (week 3-6)

A trial period with monitoring. If after 30-45 days resistance persists, the decision is clear.

4

Decision (week 6-8)

If there was a change, reclassify to Group A or B. If there was not, redirect to another area or function.

💡 Don’t confuse resistance with incapacity

A person in Group C may havemore potential than one from Group Aif the blockage is removed. But you can't wait indefinitely. Set a deadline, work on the blocking, and make the decision based on evidence.

Topic 4: Group D
4

🔄 Group D — No commercial fit

Low aptitude and low will.The diagnosis is clear: the commercial area is not the right place for this person. It's not a judgment of character - it's a confirmation of professional fitness. The best thing you can do for this person is to be honest and help them find the right path.

✓ How to drive

  • ✓ Individual, private and respectful conversation
  • ✓ Present diagnostic data
  • ✓ Highlight qualities for other functions
  • ✓ Offer support in the transition

✗ What to avoid

  • ✗ Dismiss without prior conversation
  • ✗ Publicly humiliate
  • ✗ Keeping it out of pity (harms everyone)
  • ✗ Waiting for it to “work out” without evidence

Possible destinations for Group D

🎧
Customer Success
If you have empathy but not a hunting profile
📊
Operations / Processes
If you are organized and detailed
💻
Technical Support
If you have product knowledge
📋
Administrative
If it is reliable and methodical

📊 Perspective

In an average team,10-15% of sellers are Group D. Keeping them in the wrong role is costly: unreturned salary, wasted management time, affected team morale and a vacancy filled that could have been filled by someone from Group A or B.
