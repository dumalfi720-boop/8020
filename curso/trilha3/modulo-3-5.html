📋 How to apply the assessment

The quality of the diagnosis depends both on thetool and application context. Wrong timing, wrong environment or wrong format distort the results and invalidate the entire process.

The application checklist

📅
Moment
  • → After 30-60 days of work
  • → Not in a period of crisis
  • → Not immediately after layoffs
  • → Preferably at the beginning of the quarter
🏢
Environment
  • → Reserved and private room
  • → No interruptions
  • → Calm and peaceful environment
  • → Expected duration: 30-45 min
📝
Format
  • → Individual and confidential
  • → Manager evaluates + self-evaluation
  • → Cross the two views
  • → Record everything in writing

💡 Essential tip

Make theseller self-assessment BEFOREyour assessment as a manager. Compare the two later. The divergences between the views are as revealing as the scores themselves.

💬 How to give feedback

The return and the most important momentdelicate and most importantof the entire process. Done well, it transforms the diagnosis into motivation and an action plan. Done poorly, it generates resentment and distrust.

The feedback framework

1

Context and intention (2 min)

"We carried out an assessment of the commercial profile of the entire team. The objective is not to judge anyone, but to understand where each person is in order to better invest in development."

2

Strengths first (5 min)

Start with the factors with the highest score. Show with concrete examples. "Your willingness score is 22 - very high. This shows in your proactivity and the way you deal with rejection."

3

Development areas (5 min)

Present the factors with the lowest score. Use developmental language, not criticism. "Your execution score has room to grow. Let's work on this together."

4

Joint action plan (10 min)

Build the action plan WITH the seller, not FOR the seller. Ask: "What do you think we can do to improve this?" Commitment built together has more adherence.

✓ Phrases that help

  • "Your strong point and..."
  • "This area has room to grow..."
  • "Let's work together on this..."
  • "What do you think works?"

✗ Phrases that destroy

  • "You're weak at this..."
  • "Compared to so-and-so, you..."
  • "You need to improve or..."
  • "It's always been like that..."

⚠️ Common manager mistakes when classifying

Every human being hascognitive biasesthat distort assessments. Knowing them is the first step to minimizing them. A biased diagnosis is worse than no diagnosis.

!

Halo Effect

A strong quality contaminates the entire evaluation. "He's nice, so he must be good at everything." Evaluate factor by factor, in isolation.

!

Recency bias

Just remember last month. If the seller had a bad month, he evaluates everything as bad - even if the previous 5 months were good. Use 90+ days of data.

!

Favoritism

Better evaluate who you like personally. Sympathy is not aptitude. The salesperson who has lunch with you is not necessarily the best.

!

Similarity Bias

Better evaluate who looks like you. If you were an extroverted salesperson, you may underestimate introverted salespeople who perform differently.

!

Central Tendency

Give everything a 3 so as not to make mistakes. This makes the diagnosis useless. Take position: if it is 2, put 2. If it is 5, put 5.

💡 Antidote

Useconcrete behavioral examplesto justify each note. Instead of "I think he's good at communicating," think "In the meeting on Thursday, he explained the product clearly and the customer understood it right away." Observable behavior reduces bias.

🔄 When the classification is wrong

No diagnosis is perfect.Signs that the classification needs to be revisedappear when the actual results do not match what the group predicted. Be aware of these discrepancies.

Signs of reevaluation

  • ✓ Group A that does not perform:If you are classified as natural talent but don't deliver results for 2+ months, something is wrong with the classification or context.
  • ✓ Group B that surpasses Group A:If the "constructible" is performing above the "natural", the classification may be inverted.
  • ✓ Abrupt behavior change:Salesperson who was proactive and stopped. Or that it was resistant and suddenly engages. Investigate the cause and reevaluate.
  • ✓ Significant personal event:Divorce, illness, birth of a child. External factors can temporarily or permanently change the profile.

📊 Practical rule

If the actual performance isconsistently different(more or less) than the group predicted for more than 60 days, and it's time to reevaluate. Don't wait for the quarter - anticipate it.
