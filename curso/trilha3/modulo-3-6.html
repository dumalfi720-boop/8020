📅 When to reevaluate

The diagnosis requiresfixed frequency and exception triggers. Frequency ensures that the portrait remains up to date. Triggers allow you to react quickly when something changes outside of the normal cycle.

Reevaluation calendar

Q

Quarterly (default)

Reevaluate all sellers every 90 days. It's enough time to see real evolution without being so long that you miss important changes.

M

Monthly (for Group B in ramp-up)

Group B sellers in the first 90 days of development deserve monthly monitoring to adjust the plan quickly.

Immediate reassessment triggers

📉
Sudden drop in performance
Result drops by more than 30% in 30 days without apparent cause
📈
Unexpected jump
Salesperson from Group B starts performing as Group A
🔄
Change of role/territory
New segment, new product, new portfolio
💭
Relevant personal event
Marriage, divorce, illness, birth, bereavement

💡 Rule of thumb

Diagnosis without reevaluation becomes a label.If you classify someone as "Group D" once and never reevaluate again, you're not diagnosing - you're labeling. The process only works if it is alive.

📊 How to measure progress

Each factor hasspecific indicatorsthat allow us to measure evolution objectively. Don't just rely on perception - use data.

Indicators by factor

🗣️ Aptitude — Indicators

  • → Call/meeting conversion rate in next step
  • → Customer feedback (service NPS)
  • → Assessment of peers and managers in role play
  • → Average sales cycle duration (shorter = better communication)

🔥 Will — Indicators

  • → Volume of activities (calls, emails, meetings) vs target
  • → Proactivity in prospecting (own vs received leads)
  • → Recovery speed after month is poor
  • → Voluntary participation in training and projects

📈 Learning — Indicators

  • → Time between feedback and application (the shorter the better)
  • → Reduction of repeated errors month after month
  • → Score evolution in call quality assessments
  • → Consumption of study and training materials

⚙️ Execution — Indicators

  • → Adheres to daily routine (% of activities completed)
  • → CRM update (% of records up to date)
  • → Follow-up on time (% of follow-ups on time)
  • → Goal achievement (% of goal achieved)

📊 Insight

The history of scores andmore valuable than the current score. A salesperson with a score of 15 who came from 10 is on an upward trajectory. A salesperson with a score of 20 who came from 24 is on a downward trajectory. The trend matters more than the photo.

🔀 When changing someone group

Changing someone's group andimportant decision that needs clear criteria. Without criteria, the system loses credibility. With criteria, it becomes an engine of motivation and development.

Criteria for changing groups

  • ✓ Minimum 2 consecutive assessmentswith a score compatible with the new group. One assessment can be an anomaly - two form a pattern.
  • ✓ Consistent behavioral evidencethat supports the new score. The numbers need to match what is observed on a daily basis.
  • ✓ Aligned resultswith the new group. If it goes to Group A, it needs to be delivering like Group A in terms of revenue and activities.
  • ✓ Stability— change cannot be based on a temporary peak or valley. It needs to be a trend.

↑ Group promotion

  • → Celebrate publicly (with permission)
  • → Adjust the development strategy
  • → Increase challenges and autonomy
  • → Use as an example for the team

↓ Group relegation

  • → Communicate privately
  • → Investigate the cause of the fall
  • → Redefine the development plan
  • → Offer support, not punishment

💡 Important

Mobility between groups must bevisible to the entire team(anonymously if necessary). When the team sees that it is possible to move up a group, the diagnosis becomes a motivation tool, not a labeling tool.

💎 The trail's key phrase

The entire Track 3 can be summarized in a single sentence that should guide your commercial management from now on:

Highlight phrase

"Talent helps you enter quickly, desire and learning determine who becomes good."

What does this mean in practice

1

Don't hire just for talent

The most talented salesperson in the interview may be the first to leave. Look for signs of willingness and ability to learn beyond aptitude.

2

Invest in Group B

Salespeople with high desire and learning are the best long-term investment. They evolve, stay and give back.

3

Don't ignore execution

Potential without execution and promise. Result requires daily discipline. The best salesperson is the one who does the basics every day.

4

Always reevaluate

People change. Contexts change. The diagnosis is a compass, not a fixed map. Keep it alive and updated.

📊 The result of applying this system

Managers who apply business diagnosis consistently report:40% reduction in turnover, 25% increase in productivity per salesperson e 60% reduction in ramp-up timeof new sellers. Diagnosis is not bureaucracy - it is the basis of professional commercial management.
