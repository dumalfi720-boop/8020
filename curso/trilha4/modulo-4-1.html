🏗️ The role of the commercial manager

The commercial manager is not a goal-getter — he is the architect who designs the conditions for results to happen in a consistent and sustainable way.

Topic 1
1

🎯 It’s not just about setting a goal

Most commercial managers were promoted for being a good salesperson. The problem?Selling and managing are completely different skills. The manager who only demands goals is doing the minimum — and the minimum does not generate high-performance teams.

Main Box

What does the manager as an architect do differently?

  • ✓ Design the sales process before demanding results
  • ✓ Diagnose profiles before distributing wallets
  • ✓ Creates conditions for the seller to perform, not just demand performance
  • ✓ Measures activities and behaviors, not just final results
Comparison Grid

✓ Architect Manager

  • "We will adjust the process for this profile"
  • "What objection stuck? Let's practice this"
  • "This lead is best for seller X"
  • "Let's simulate this scenario first"

✗ Billing Manager

  • "Where's the goal? You're late"
  • "Make more calls"
  • "The problem is motivation"
  • "If you turn around, you already know how to sell"
Practical Tip

💡 Practical Exercise

List your 5 most frequent activities as a manager in the last week. Classify each one: "billing" or "construction". If more than 60% is billing, you are operating as a bill collector, not as an architect. The first step is awareness.

Topic 2
2

🔍 Diagnose, position, train

The three essential functions of the commercial manager form a continuous cycle. First youdiagnosethe profile of each seller. Afterpositionseach in the ideal role and portfolio. Finally,trainwith the method appropriate to the profile.

Detail Box

The Effective Manager Cycle

1

Diagnose

Observe, listen to calls, analyze metrics. Understand: is this salesperson talented, trainable or resilient? What are your strengths and gaps?

2

Position

Allocate to the right role: closer, SDR, farmer, hunter. Distribute a portfolio suited to the profile. Place it at the customer level where it has the most impact.

3

To train

Develop with an appropriate method: autonomy for the talented, script and monitoring for the trainable, repositioning for the resistant.

Insight

📊 Perspective

Most managers jump straight to “coaching” without diagnosing. Result: generic training that doesn't work for anyone.Diagnosis is the most important step— and the most ignored.

Topic 3
3

🔀 Match seller with customer

O match between seller and customerIt is one of the most powerful and least used levers in commercial management. It's not about who sells more — it's about who sells best to which type of customer.

Concept Box

The Logic of Crossing

Each level of customer awareness requires a different type of approach. And each salesperson profile has natural strengths that fit better at certain levels.

⭐
Talented → Low conscientiousness
📏
Trainable → Media awareness
🔄
Resistant → High awareness (support)
Tip

💡 Rule of Thumb

Before redistributing your portfolio, make a simple map: list your salespeople in one column and the types of customers in the other. Connect each seller to the type where they historically convert the most. You will discover patterns that were hidden in the data.

⚖️ The manager who trains vs the manager who charges

There are two commercial management philosophies. THEchargeIt works in the short term: pressure generates movement. THEtrainingworks in the long term: development generates capacity. The best managers combine the two, but prioritize training.

Box Visual

Impact over time

📢

Quick result, fall later
← Result curve →
Combining is ideal
🌱
Form
Slower, exponential growth
Comparison

Manager who only demands

  • → High turnover (above 40%/year)
  • → Team depends on external pressure
  • → When the manager leaves, results collapse
  • → Frequent burnout in the team

Manager who forms

  • → Controlled turnover (below 15%)
  • → Autonomous and self-motivated team
  • → Results are maintained even without the manager
  • → Culture of continuous learning
Insight

📊 Why does this matter?

The cost of replacing a salesperson is estimated at 6 to 9 months' salary (recruitment, training, ramp-up). A manager who saves moneyhundreds of thousands of reaisper year on retention only.

5

📋 The daily life of an effective commercial manager

An effective manager does not improvise his day. He has astructured routinewhich ensures that diagnosis, positioning and training happen consistently, not when there is “time left”.

Main Box

Effective manager's weekly routine

📊
Monday: Pipeline Review
Analyze funnel, identify bottlenecks
🎧
Tuesday/Wednesday: Listening and 1:1
Listen to calls, individual coaching
🎭
Thursday: Role Play
Group simulations, objection practice
📝
Friday: Review and Planning
Results of the week, plan for next week
Tip

💡 The good news

You don't need to implement everything at once. Start withone activity per week: the pipeline review on Monday. When it becomes a habit, add 1:1. Build the routine brick by brick.
