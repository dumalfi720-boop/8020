💎 Why this group is the most important

In any commercial team, trainable people represent60 to 70% of the team. They don't have the natural brilliance of the talented, but they have something even more valuable:discipline, willingness to learn and coachability. Invest in this group and where the manager generates the greatest impact.

The mathematics of investment

⭐
Talented
15-20% of the team
They already perform alone
📏
Trainable

Greater management ROI
🔄
Resistant
10-20% of the team
Need repositioning

📊 Perspective

If you improve the performance of trainables in just20%, the total impact on revenue is greater than doubling the result of a single talent. The numbers don't lie: the ROI is in the trainable.

Topic 2
2

📝 Script as a crutch that becomes a skill

The script has a bad reputation because many use it wrong.Script is not to be read robotically — it is to be internalized until it becomes second nature. Think like a musician who first reads the score and then plays from memory.

The 4 phases of the script

1

Reading

The salesperson reads the script during the first few calls. It sounds artificial, but it ensures that the right points are covered.

2

Reference

The seller consults the script only on the points he has not yet mastered. He already speaks naturally for the most part.

3

Internalization

The script is in your head. The seller follows the structure without reading, adding his personal style.

4

Adaptation

The salesperson adapts the script to each situation, maintaining the essence but personalizing the approach.

💡 Practical Exercise

Create a 60-second opening script. Make the trainable read10 times out loud. By the 5th time it sounds more natural. In 10th, you almost don't need to look. This is the way: repetition becomes fluency.

Topic 3
3

👁️ Close follow-up

The trainable needsfrequent and specific feedback. It's not micromanagement — it's coaching. The difference: micromanagement controls, coaching develops. Close monitoring at the beginning exponentially accelerates learning.

Recommended tracking cadence

📅
Week 1-4: Diary
Listen to calls, immediate feedback, route correction
📊
Week 5-8: 3x/week
Pipeline review, role play, structured feedback
🎯
Week 9-12: 2x/week
1:1 weekly + metrics review
🚀
After 90 days: Weekly
Regular 1:1 with a focus on continuous development

✓ Effective coaching

  • "On the 10am call, when the customer said X, you responded Y. An alternative would be..."
  • "Your conversion rate went up 5% this week. What did you do differently?"

✗ Micromanagement

  • "Why didn't you make 50 calls today?"
  • "You need to improve" (without specifying what)
Topic 4
4

🎭 Weekly role play

No athlete competes without training. No musician plays without rehearsing.Why should salespeople go for real calls without simulating them first?The weekly role play and training that separates amateur teams from professionals.

How to set up an effective role play

1

Choose real scenario

Use objections and situations that actually happened that week. Don't invent scenarios — use reality.

2

Simulate in pairs

One is the seller, the other is the customer. The "client" must be challenging but realistic. 5-7 minutes per round.

3

Immediate feedback

After each round: what worked, what to adjust, what to try differently. Specific and actionable feedback.

💡 Rule of Thumb

Book45 minutes per weekfor role play. Does it seem like little? In a year, there are almost 40 hours of deliberate practice. Teams that do weekly role play convert on average 23% more than teams that don't.

Topic 5
5

📐 Very clear process

The trainable thrives onabsolute clarity. He needs to know exactly: how many calls to make, when to send follow-ups, how to register in the CRM, when to escalate to the manager. Ambiguity is the enemy of this profile.

Clear end-to-end process

  • ✓ Daily activity:X calls, Y emails, Z follow-ups — specific numbers
  • ✓ Follow-up cadence:Day 1, Day 3, Day 7, Day 14 — with template for each stage
  • ✓ Qualification criteria:When is the lead qualified? Clear checklist with 5 criteria
  • ✓ Escalation:When to involve the manager? Clear rules for when to ask for help
  • ✓ CRM:What to record, when to record, in which field — no room for doubt

📊 The result

A trainable with a clear process generatespredictable results. You know exactly what to expect because you know exactly what he's doing. Predictability is the greatest gift a manager can give to the board.
