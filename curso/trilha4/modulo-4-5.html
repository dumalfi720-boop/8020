🚪 When to redirect someone

One of the manager's most difficult and most important decisions: recognizing when it won't work and acting with fairness, clarity and respect.

⚠️ Signs that it won't work

They existobjective indicatorsthat the investment is not generating a return. The manager needs to separate emotion from data and make decisions based on evidence, not hope.

The 7 warning signs

  • 1 Activity metrics falling consistently for 3+ weeks
  • 2 Repeated resistance to feedback — apply once, return to old pattern
  • 3 Lack of evolution after specific and documented interventions
  • 4 Negative impact on team morale — contagious demotivation
  • 5 Constant external justifications — “the market”, “the leads”, “the product”
  • 6 Does not attend training or participates without engagement
  • 7 The person himself shows unhappiness or lack of interest in the role

📊 Perspective

Don't confuseslow learning with impossibility. Some trainables take longer. The difference is in the direction: if there is progress (even slow), keep investing. If there is no direction for improvement, it is time to act.

Topic 2
2

⏳ How much time to give

The fair deadline depends on the context, but there arepractical referencesthat balance investment with results. The secret is the intermediate milestones — don't wait 90 days to evaluate for the first time.

Timeline with milestones

30

30 days: Attitude and activity

Evaluate: are you doing the activities? Does it demonstrate a desire to learn? Apply feedback? If not, it would be a conversation.

60

60 days: Measurable progress

Evaluate: have metrics improved? Have skills evolved? If there is no measurable progress, formal action plan.

90

90 days: Final decision

Assess: Did you meet the plan milestones? If yes, continue. If not, redirect or disconnect clearly.

💡 Rule of Thumb

Document everything. Every feedback, every milestone, every result.Documentation protects you, the company and the person. When the decision comes, everyone knows it was fair because the process was transparent.

Topic 3
3

💬 How to communicate without demotivating

The redirection conversation is one of the most difficult in management. Done badly, it destroys a person's self-esteem and creates enemies.Done well, it opens doors and preserves dignity.

Difficult conversation framework

  • 1 Recognize effort:"I see you dedicated yourself and tried. That matters."
  • 2 Present data:"The numbers show that this function is not bringing the results we need."
  • 3 Focus on the future:"I believe your skills are best suited to..."
  • 4 Offer way:"These are the options we have. Which one makes the most sense to you?"

✓ Effective communication

  • Focus on facts, not judgments
  • Presents concrete alternatives
  • Listen to the person's perspective
  • Preserves dignity in all scenarios

✗ Common mistakes

  • "You're not good for sales"
  • Compare with other sellers
  • Doing the talking in front of others
  • Do not offer any alternative

Not every sales exit needs to be a company exit. The manager whomaps possible destinationsretains talent, saves hiring costs and preserves institutional knowledge.

For those who are good with data and analysis

💡 The good news

The cost of relocating internally and5 to 10x smallerthan firing and hiring. Furthermore, the person already knows the culture, the product and the customers. The manager who redirects well becomes a reference in the company.
