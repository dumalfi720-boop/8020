🙈 Strategy for the Unconscious Public

Learn to reach and awaken those who don't know they have a problem. The largest available market — and the one most ignored by most businesses.

Topic 1

🤷 Who is the unconscious

The unconscious public is the one whodon't realize there's a problem. He lives his routine thinking that everything is "normal" — that losing customers is natural, that making little revenue is a phase, that not having a process is "just plain". This is the largest segment of the market and the most ignored.

Main Box

Characteristics of the unconscious public

  • ✓ You don’t look for solutions because you don’t know you need one
  • ✓ Accept the current situation as “normal” or “it’s just the same”
  • ✓ Does not respond to direct offers - simply ignores
  • ✓ Represents 60-80% of the total potential market
Comparison Grid

✓ What he thinks

  • "My business is going, slowly but going"
  • "It's always been like this, I don't need to change"
  • "Big companies are the ones who need this"
  • "I don't have time to mess with this right now"

✗ What he doesn't see

  • Loses R$30 thousand/month due to lack of process
  • Competitors are growing 3x faster
  • His team is unmotivated and he doesn't realize
  • The market is changing and he will be left behind

💡 Practical Exercise

List 5 problems your customers had BEFORE they came to you — problems they didn't know were problems. These are the entry points for the audience unaware of your niche.

Topic 2
2

📚 Education as a strategy

You can't sell to people who don't know they need to buy. The only effective strategy for the unconscious andeducate without selling. Create content that makes people recognize the problem before they know there is a solution.

The Education Framework for the Unconscious

1

Mirror the situation

"Do you feel like you work hard but the results aren't coming?" — make the person recognize themselves

2

Reveal the hidden problem

"This is not normal. There is a specific reason." — name the problem

3

Plant the seed

“There’s a different way to do it.” — open the door without pushing

📊 Important information

Educational content generates3x more engagementthan top-of-the-funnel promotional content. The unconscious public doesn't want to be sold — it wants to be understood.

Topic 3
3

🎯 Types of content that work

For the unconscious public, technical content doesn't work. It does not search for specialized terms. What works arestories, shocking data and comparisonsthat break the perception of normality.

Formats that work for unconscious people

📖
Real stories
"Joao thought it was normal to lose 40% of leads..."
📊
Shocking data
"78% of companies lose customers due to lack of follow-up"
⚖️
Before vs After
"Company A without process vs Company B with process"
🪞
Quiz/Checklist
"Do you make these 5 mistakes without knowing it?"

Example of a post for the unconscious

"I thought losing 3 out of 10 customers was normal.

Until I discovered that companies my size lose on average 1 in 10.

I was losing 3x more — and thought I was 'within normal'.

The problem was not the market. It was the lack of a process that I didn't even know I needed."

→ This type of post generates identification without selling anything. The reader thinks: "could it be that I too...?"

💡 Golden Rule

For unconscious,never mention your product. The goal is to make the person think "this happens to me" — not "someone is trying to sell me something."

📡 Optimal Channels

The unconscious publicNot looking for a solution on Google. He doesn't type "how to solve my sales problem" because he doesn't even know he has that problem. You need to go to him on the channels where he is already.

📊 Strategic insight

The logic is simple: unconscious people don't search, so you need tointerrupt. But the interruption needs to be with value, not with supply. The content is your "permission to exist" in their feed.

Topic 5
5

💬 Practical examples of communication

Let's see real examples of communication that works for the unconscious audience. Each example follows the principle:mirror, reveal, plant seed.

Example 1: Identification Post (Instagram)

Headline:

"5 signs that you are losing money without realizing it"

Body:

1. Your customers disappear after the first purchase
2. You don't know how much it costs to acquire 1 customer
3. Your sales team has no script
4. You never measured the rate of return
5. You think "that's just the way it is"

CTA:

"If you scored 3 or more, you don't have a market problem. You have a process problem."

Example 2: Short Video (Reels/TikTok)

Itinerary (30s):

"I had a store that had a turnover of R$50,000 a month. I thought I was doing well. Until I discovered that stores my size had a turnover of R$150,000 with the same structure. The problem wasn't a lack of customers. It was a lack of process for the customer who already came in."

→ No sales CTA. Just the story. The comment is full of "what do you mean?"

Example 3: Article / Carousel (LinkedIn)

Title:

"The invisible cost that no one measures: how much you lose by not having follow-up"

Angle:

Use research data + real history + monthly loss calculation. People leave thinking “I need to check this in my business”.

💡 Practical Exercise

Create 3 pieces of content for an audience unaware of YOUR niche: 1 identification post, 1 short video script and 1 article title. Use the mirror → reveal → plant seed framework.
