😣 Strategy for Awareness of the Problem

The person already knows they have pain. Now you need to deepen this pain, show the consequences and generate internal urgency for her to seek a solution.

Topic 1
1

🔍 What characterizes this level

The awareness of the problemyou know something is wrongbut he doesn't know what to do. He feels the pain — loss of customers, stagnant revenue, unmotivated team — but has not connected this pain to a specific solution.

Signs that someone is at this level

  • ✓ Google search: “why don’t my customers come back”
  • ✓ Read articles about "how to improve sales" without depth
  • ✓ Talk to other businesspeople about difficulties
  • ✓ Feeling frustrated but don't know where to start

Unconscious (previous level)

  • → "I don't have a problem"
  • → Does not search for anything
  • → Thinks everything is normal
  • → Need to be polite

Problem Aware (this level)

  • → "I know something is wrong"
  • → Search for information
  • → Feel the pain
  • → Need direction

2

💉 Deepen the pain

The person knows they have pain, but they tolerate it. Your mission andshow the real cost of not acting. It's not manipulation — it's clarity. Quantifying the loss transforms tolerance into urgency.

Example of copy that deepens the pain

"You know you're losing customers. What you might not know is HOW MUCH you're losing.

Do this math: how many leads did you receive this month? How many became customers? The difference — the ones that didn't turn over — is money you left on the table.

If each lead is worth R$500 and you lose 10 per month, that's R$5,000 evaporating. Per month. Every month."

💡 Practical Exercise

Calculate the “cost of inaction” of your ideal customer. How much does he lose per month, per year and in 5 years by not solving the problem you solve? Use these numbers in all your communication.

Topic 3
3

🔮 Show consequence

In addition to quantifying the current loss, you need toproject the future without solution. Where will the person be in 6 months, 1 year, 5 years if they do nothing? Negative projection is one of the most powerful motivators there is.

Timeline of Inaction

😐
Today
"I can handle it"
😟
6 months
"Starting to weigh in"
😰
1 year
"Crisis installed"

Example of consequence projection

"Imagine 1 year from now, in the same situation. Your competitors have already implemented processes. Your best salespeople have gone to more organized companies. Your revenue has fallen by 20% and you work more hours than ever.

It's not pessimism. This is what happens when a known problem is not addressed."

📊 Psychological principle

Studies show that people are2x more motivated to avoid losses than to seek gains(loss aversion, Kahneman). Projecting negative consequences is more effective than promising benefits.

Topic 4
4

⏰ Generate internal urgency

Internal urgency is different from sales pressure. It's not "buy now or lose the discount". And make the person conclude alone:"I need to solve this now". Motivation comes from within, not from without.

✗ External emergency (handling)

  • "Only today! Last chance!"
  • "Only 2 spots left!" (lie)
  • "If you don't buy now, never again"
  • Generates distrust and regret

✓ Internal urgency (clarity)

  • "Each month without a solution costs R$5,000"
  • "Your competitors have already started"
  • "The best moment was yesterday. The second best is today"
  • Generates conviction and conscious decision

3 Internal Urgency Triggers

  • 1. Accumulated cost:Show how much you have already lost and how much you will lose if you don't act
  • 2. Comparison with peers:Show what others at the same level are already doing
  • 3. Window of opportunity:Show that the market is changing and those who don't adapt are left behind

💡 Golden Rule

The best urgency is the one where the customer says"I need to resolve this"before you say anything about your product. If he reached this conclusion alone, the sale is practically done.

Topic 5
5

💬 Examples of content and approach

Content for those aware of the problem needsdeepen, quantify and direct. Unlike the unconscious (where you just mirror), here you can be more direct about the problem and its consequences.

Example 1: Nurturing Email

Subject: The cost of each lead you lose

"João, you told me that you receive 100 leads per month and convert 8. This means that 92 interested people leave.

If each customer is worth R$2,000, you are leaving R$184,000 on the table. Every month.

The good news: companies that implement a simple follow-up process increase conversion from 8% to 18% on average.

Want to know how? Reply to this email."

Example 2: In-depth Post (LinkedIn)

Title: "3 numbers that every businessman should measure — and most don't"

1. Customer acquisition cost (CAC)
2. Lifetime Value (LTV)
3. Churn rate (loss of customers)

"If you don't know these 3 numbers, you're driving in the dark. And in the dark, you crash."

Example 3: Webinar / Live

Title: "The 5 leaks that drain your company's revenue"

Format: 40 minutes of pure content showing the problems + 10 minutes of Q&A + 5 minutes mentioning that there is a way to solve them (without selling directly).

→ The objective is not to sell. And make the person leave thinking "I need to solve this NOW".

💡 Practical Exercise

Create a nurturing email for your problem-aware ideal client. Use the formula: identify the pain → quantify the loss → project consequences → open a door (without selling).
