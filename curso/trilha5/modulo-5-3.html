MODULE 5.3

🔎 Solution Aware Strategy

The person knows that there are solutions but they don't know you yet. Position yourself as the reference she was looking for.

Topic 1
1

🧭 What characterizes this level

The conscious awareness of the solution has already passed the pain phase. He knows thatThere are ways to solve your problem. He is researching options, comparing approaches, reading reviews. But he still doesn't know you — or knows you superficially.

Typical behavior

  • ✓Search "best tool for X", "how to choose Y"
  • ✓Compare 3 to 5 options simultaneously
  • ✓Read reviews, watch comparison videos, ask for recommendations
  • ✓Values ​​authority and credentials more than price

📊 Strategic insight

At this level,generic content no longer works. People want depth. You want to see that you master the subject more than others. This is where authority becomes a bargaining chip.

Topic 2
2

⭐ Show differences

It's not enough to say you're good. You need to showWhy is your approach different?. What do you do that others don't do? What is its unique mechanism? What specific result do you deliver?

Differentiation Framework

1

Unique Mechanism

"We use the 8020 Method — we focus on the 20% of actions that generate 80% of the results"

2

Specific Result

"Our customers increase conversion by an average of 127% in the first 90 days"

3

Specific Audience

"We work exclusively with service companies from R$50k to R$500k/month"

💡 Exercise

Complete: "Different from others [your category], we [your differential] what allows [specific result] for [specific audience]."

Topic 3
3

🤝 Position without attacking competitors

The temptation is to speak badly of the competitor.Resist. Instead of attacking, compare solution categories. Instead of saying "they are bad", say "there are 3 different approaches and we chose C for these reasons".

✗ Aggressive positioning

  • "Others don't know what they're doing"
  • "Be careful with those who charge cheaply"
  • "We're the only ones who..."
  • Generates antipathy and distrust

✓ Smart positioning

  • "There are 3 approaches: A, B and C"
  • "We chose C because..."
  • "For profile X, approach C works best"
  • Generates trust and respect

Smart comparison example

"To improve sales, there are basically 3 ways:

A) More traffic:Invest more in advertisements to bring more leads. It works, but it's expensive and has a limit.

B) More sellers:Hire more people. It works, but it increases fixed costs and complexity.

C) Best process:Convert more of the leads that already arrive. Low cost, quick results, scalable.

We focus on C because and where the highest ROI is for most companies."

Topic 4
4

🏆 Authority content

Authority is not declared — ifdemonstrates. Authority content shows that you have a deeper understanding of the subject than most. It's not about bragging — it's about teaching what only those who know a lot can teach.

Types of Authority Content

📊
Deep analysis
Take a common theme and break it down with data and nuances
📋
Case study
Show real result with process details
🔧
Own framework
Create and name your method or approach
🎤
Reasoned opinion
Go against common sense with solid arguments

💡 Rule of Thumb

The authority test: if anyone in your market could have written that content,not authoritative content. Authority comes from the unique perspective that only deep experience allows.

Topic 5
5

💬 Examples of communication for this level

For the solution conscious, the content needs to bedeep, comparative and demonstrate mastery. The person is choosing — your content needs to be proof that you are the best option.

Example 1: Comparative Article

Title: "Consulting vs Course vs Mentoring: which approach works for service companies?"

Format: compare the 3 paths with pros, cons and ideal profile for each one. In the end, explain why you chose path X for profile Y.

Example 2: Detailed Case Study

Title: "How company X went from making R$80k to R$240k/month in 4 months"

Format: context → diagnosis → solution applied → results → learning. With real numbers and details of the process.

→ Detailed cases are the most powerful weapon at this level. Nothing convinces more than documented results.

Example 3: Positioning Webinar

Title: "The mistake that 90% of companies make when trying to scale sales"

Format: 1h live. 40min of in-depth content demonstrating authority + 15min of case + 5min of how to work with you.

💡 Practical Exercise

Create 1 detailed case study of a real client. Include: initial situation, diagnosis, what was done, numerical results and client testimony. This will be your most valuable asset.
