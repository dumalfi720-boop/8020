🛒 Strategy for Product Awareness

The person knows your offer and is comparing. Now is the time for social proof, risk reduction and trust building to close the sale.

Topic 1

🏷️ What characterizes this level

The product consciousalready know you and your offer. Have you visited your website, seen your content, maybe even talked to your team. But he hasn't bought it yet. This is the time for final assessment — comparing, measuring risk, seeking safety.

What's going through his head

  • ✓"Will it work for MY case?"
  • ✓"What if I invest and it doesn't pay off?"
  • ✓"Other people had real results?"
  • ✓"Can I implement it?"

📊 Critical data

67% of qualified leadsthat don't convert is because there was a lack of social proof or risk reduction, not because the price was high. The objection "it's expensive" almost always hides "I'm not sure it works".

Topic 2
2

⭐ Social proof

Social proof and the evidence thatother people have already had results with you. It is the most powerful trigger at this level because it transfers confidence from those who have already purchased to those who are thinking about buying.

Social Proof Hierarchy (from most to least powerful)

1. Testimonial video— Customer talking emotionally about the result
2. Detailed case with numbers— Before/after with verifiable data
3. Message printout— WhatsApp, email, DM from the customer thanking
4. Written statement— Text with name, photo and company
5. Number of clients/results— "More than 500 companies served"

💡 Exercise

Collect at least 3 customer testimonials TODAY. Ask permission to use. A 30-second video of a satisfied customer is worth more than 10 pages of copy.

Topic 3
3

🛡️ Reduction of perceived risk

Even with social proof, fear persists:"What if it doesn't work for ME?". Your mission is to eliminate this fear with risk reduction mechanisms — guarantees, tests, demonstrations.

Risk Reduction Mechanisms

🔄
Money-back guarantee
"30 days or your money back, no questions asked"
🆓
Trial period
"7 days free to test before deciding"
🎥
Live demonstration
"Book a session to see how it works"
📊
Result guarantee
"If we don't achieve X result, we're still free"

📊 Principle

Risk inversion:transfer the risk from the buyer to the seller. When the customer feels that the risk is theirs (seller), not theirs (buyer), the barrier drops drastically.

Topic 4
4

🏆 Authority and trust

At this level, authority and trust are thelast filters before purchase. The person needs to feel that they are in good hands — that you have the experience, track record and credibility to deliver what you promise.

Elements of Trust

  • ✓Market time:"10 years working in consultative sales"
  • ✓Customer volume:"More than 300 companies served"
  • ✓Recognition:"Speaker at X events", "Mentioned in Y publications"
  • ✓Partners:"Official Partner of Z", "Certified by W"
  • ✓Transparency:Show behind the scenes, mistakes learned, humanize

💡 Golden Rule

Trust is built inmonths but destroys itself in seconds. Never promise what you cannot deliver. It's better to promise less and surprise than to promise too much and disappoint.

Topic 5
5

💬 Examples of approaches for this level

For the product conscious, communication isfocused on eliminating objections and generating security. Each piece of content must answer a specific question that prevents purchase.

Example 1: Follow-Up Email

Subject: What Maria did in the first 30 days

"João, I know you are evaluating. Let me tell you what happened to Maria, who had exactly the same company profile as you:

Week 1: Implemented the follow-up process
Week 2: Started measuring conversion rate
Week 3: Adjusted the script based on data
Week 4: Conversion rose from 8% to 14%

In 30 days, R$23,000 more in the cash register. With the same team.

Do you want me to show you how this would work in your company?"

Example 2: Strategic FAQ Page

Each FAQ answers a real objection:

“Does it work for small businesses?”→ Case of a company with 3 employees

"How long does it take to see results?"→ Average of 30-45 days with data

"What if it doesn't work?"→ Detailed warranty

"Do I need a technical team?"→ No, the process is simple

💡 Practical Exercise

List the 10 most common objections your leads raise before purchasing. Create a response for each with social proof or data. These answers become your FAQ, your emails and your sales scripts.
