Integrating team and strategy

Marketing, sales and management are not separate departments — they are cogs in a single machine. In this module, you learn how to orchestrate everything: which salesperson profile works at each level, when to scale, and how to set up the integrated model.

5 Topics ~45 min Strategic Integration
Topic 1
🎯

1. Which seller profile best suits each level

Not every salesperson is suitable for every stage of the funnel. Talent shines in closing. The trainable performs well with script in the top and middle. The resistant can generate value in pre-sale. The key is to position each person where their profile generates the most results.

Matrix: Profile x Level of awareness

Profile Top (Unconscious) Middle (Conscious) Background (Ready)
Group A — Talented It works, but it's wasteful Shine Shines a lot
Group B — Trainable Great with script Good with process Need support
Group C — Resistant Pre-sale and content Limited Not recommended
Talented (Group A)

Place in the middle and bottom of the funnel. And who converts hot leads and closes complex deals. Wasting talent at the top and how to use Ferrari to go to market.

Trainable (Group B)

Best in the top and middle with a well-defined script. Qualifies leads, performs standardized diagnosis, schedules with the closer. Evolves to middle/deep with training.

Resistant (Group C)

If you can't fire, reposition. Content, pre-sale, initial qualification. Structured tasks where personality does not interfere so much in the conversation.

Topic 2
📢

2. The role of marketing at each level

Marketing does not exist to “make a pretty post”. Marketing exists to move people from one level of consciousness to the next. Each level requires a different type of content and action.

TOP

Education and identification

The person doesn't know they have a problem. Marketing at the top needs to make her notice. Educational, provocative content that generates "wow, that's me".

Formats that work
  • ▸ Educational posts on social media
  • ▸ Short videos with quick diagnosis
  • ▸ Free quizzes and assessments
  • ▸ Blog posts with market data
Objective

Make the person identify with the problem. Don't sell anything. Do not mention product. Just generate awareness that something needs to change.

QUITE

Authority and proof

The person knows they have the problem. Now you need to believe that you can solve it. Marketing in the medium builds authority and shows results.

Formats that work
  • ▸ Detailed success stories
  • ▸ Webinars with in-depth content
  • ▸ Comparisons (before/after)
  • ▸ Testimonials and social proof
Objective

Build trust. Prove that it works for people like her. Generate desire for the solution — not for the offer yet.

BOTTOM

Offer and urgency

The person wants to buy. Marketing ultimately removes the last barriers and creates the right moment for decision. This is where the direct offer comes in.

Formats that work
  • ▸ Sales pages with clear offer
  • ▸ Launch emails with deadline
  • ▸ Guarantees and risk reversal
  • ▸ Limited time bonus
Objective

Convert. Get rid of inertia. Create real (not fake) urgency. The lead is already ready — marketing just needs to push the last step.

Rule of thumb

Marketing nurtures, sales converts. When marketing tries to sell too early, it burns the lead. When sales tries to nurture, it wastes time. Each one in their role.

Topic 3
🤝

3. The role of the seller at each level

The seller does not need to be at every stage. In fact, putting salespeople in the wrong place is costly and disruptive. Here you learn exactly when the human enters and when he doesn't.

Seller map in the funnel

TOP — Salesperson’s role: almost none

At the top, the cost of placing a seller is very high and the return is low. The person doesn't yet know they have a problem — they're not ready to talk to a salesperson.

Who works: Automation (email, chatbot, content). If you need a human, use Group C for pre-sales or automated qualification.
MEDIUM — Seller’s role: consultant

The person knows they have the problem and is looking for solutions. The salesperson here is a consultant — he diagnoses, understands the situation, educates about options and builds trust.

Who works: Group B with defined script and process. Group A in higher value businesses. Consultative calls, diagnostic calls.
FUND — Seller’s role: closer

The person wants to buy. The seller here is the closer — resolves final objections, presents the proposal, negotiates and closes. Every minute spent closer at the bottom is worth 10x more than at the top.

Who works: Group A priority. Group B trained to close simpler deals. Proposal meetings, negotiation, final follow-up.

When the human enters and when he does not enter

Human ENTER when:
  • ✓ Lead demonstrated active interest
  • ✓ Business is complex or high value
  • ✓ Customer needs customization
  • ✓ There is an emotional objection to resolve
  • ✓ Competitor is in the running
Human DOES NOT enter when:
  • ✗ Lead is still cold (top of the funnel)
  • ✗ Information can be automated
  • ✗ Volume is too loud to meet 1:1
  • ✗ Average ticket does not justify the cost
  • ✗ Sequence of emails resolves

4. When to scale the operation

Escalating prematurely is the quickest way to break a commercial operation. Climbing late is the quickest way to miss an opportunity. Here you learn to read the signs and diagnose the right moment.

Diagnosis: where is the bottleneck?

🔍
Full funnel + low conversion = needs more (or better) closers

You have many leads coming in but few close. The problem isn't volume — it's conversion. Solutions: train the current team, hire closers (Group A), or improve the closing script.

Signal: conversion rate below your segment benchmark for 2+ consecutive months.
🔍
Empty funnel + idle team = needs more top

Their salespeople are good but there is no one to talk to. The problem is lead generation. Solutions: invest more in marketing, open new acquisition channels, create partnerships.

Sign: salespeople having fewer than 10 conversations per week or a stagnant pipeline.
🔍
Full funnel + good conversation + exhausted team = time to hire

Everything works but I don't care about the volume. This is the only scenario where hiring is the right answer. First validate the process, then multiply.

Sign: lead response time increasing, follow-ups being forgotten, salespeople asking for help.

Scalability rule

1. First: process.Document and standardize what works. If it is not documented, it cannot be replicated.
2. Second: metrics.Know exactly your numbers: cost per lead, conversion rate per stage, average ticket, sales cycle.
3. Third: people.Just hire now. Hire to replicate something that already works, not to invent something new.

🏗️

5. The integrated model: marketing + sales + management

The commercial manager is not just a “head of salespeople” — he is the orchestrator of the entire system. It connects marketing (which generates awareness), sales (which converts) and management (which aligns everything). When these three work together, the result is exponential.

Visual flow: the integrated model

MARKETING
Generates awareness
Attracts → Educates → Nourishes
→
SALES
Convert
Diagnose → Propose → Close
→
RESULT
Customer
Revenue → Retention → Indication
MANAGEMENT
Orchestrates everything: aligns marketing + sales, measures results, corrects route
Marketing delivers to Sales:
  • ▸ Qualified leads (not just names)
  • ▸ Information about level of consciousness
  • ▸ Content that the lead has already consumed
  • ▸ Engagement data
Sales feeds back to Marketing:
  • ▸ Most frequent objections
  • ▸ Profile of those who buy vs those who don’t buy
  • ▸ Quality of leads by channel
  • ▸ Language the customer uses
Management connects the two:
  • ▸ Weekly marketing + sales meeting
  • ▸ Shared metrics dashboard
  • ▸ Structured feedback loop
  • ▸ Aligned goals (not conflicting)

The most common mistake: silos

Marketing complains that sales don't convert. Sales complains that marketing sends bad leads. Management does not speak to either of them. Result: everyone is frustrated and the customer notices the disconnect.

The solution is simple:a weekly 15-minute meeting between marketing and sales, mediated by management. Agenda: what marketing sent, how sales received it, what needs to change. This single ritual resolves 80% of conflicts.
