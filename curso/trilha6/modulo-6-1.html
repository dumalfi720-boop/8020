Topic 1

🔍 Diagnosis before training

The most common mistake in training andteach without knowing what is missing. Before putting together any program, you need to map out where each person is. Diagnosis is not optional - it is the foundation of everything.

How to make the diagnosis

  • ✓ Listen to recordings of real customer service from each seller
  • ✓ Observe 3-5 live calls without interfering
  • ✓ Analyze individual metrics from the last 30 days
  • ✓ Ask the seller: "where do you have the most difficulty?"

✓ With diagnosis

  • "Joao doesn't ask diagnostic questions"
  • "Maria skips the value stage"
  • "Peter freezes in his objections"
  • Efficient surgical training

✗ No diagnosis

  • "Let's train everyone the same"
  • "Today's theme is motivation"
  • "Watch this sales video"
  • Generic and without impact

💡 Practical Exercise

Choose 3 sellers. Listen to 2 sessions of each. Write down in a table: (1) Did you ask questions? (2) Did you identify pain? (3) Did it present value? (4) Did you get around the objection? (5) Did you try to close? You will have the diagnosis in 1 hour.

Topic 2
2

🎯 Set clear goals

"Improve sales" is not the objective. And desire. Purpose and:"In 30 days, 100% of the team will ask the 4 diagnostic questions in all consultations". The difference is that one can be measured and the other cannot.

Training Objectives Framework

S

Specific

"Ask the 4 diagnostic questions" (not "provide better service")

M

Measurable

"In 100% of cases" (not "in the majority")

A

Attainable

"4 questions" is possible for any trained salesperson

R

Relevant

Diagnosis directly improves conversion

T

Temporal

"In 30 days" (not "soon")

📊 Perspective

Companies with clear training objectives have3x more chanceto see results in the numbers. Not because the training is better, but because they know what they are looking for.

Topic 3
3

🧱 Minimum viable structure

You don't need an e-learning platform, professional handbooks or an HR department. THEminimum viable structureA training program fits into 3 elements that you can start tomorrow.

The 3 pillars of minimal training

📚
Theme of the Day
One concept per session
🎯
Guided Practice
Apply instantly
💬
Feedback
Fix immediately

Example of a 15-minute session

0-3 min Review the concept: "Today we will practice diagnostic questions"
3-10 min Double simulation: one plays the seller, the other the customer
10-15 min Group feedback: what worked, what to adjust

💡 Rule of Thumb

If you're expecting to have everything perfect to start, you'll never start.Start with the minimum and improve every week. An imperfect program that runs is infinitely better than a perfect program that never gets off the ground.

Topic 4
4

📅 Implementation calendar

A training calendar turns intention intocommitment. When it's on the calendar, it happens. When it's "for when" it never happens. Time structure is what separates shows that work from shows that die in the second week.

Weekly calendar template

Day Activity Duration Responsible
Second Microtraining: theme of the week 15 min Manager
Third Practice in pairs 15 min Doubles
Fourth Role play with feedback 30 min Manager
Fifth Review of real services 15 min Team
Friday Metrics review + celebration 15 min Manager

📊 Quick account

Weekly total:1h30 training. In a month: 6 hours. In a quarter: 18 hours. More than 90% of companies invest in an entire year. And without taking the team out of operation.
