MODULE 6.2

👥 Profile training

Each group deserves different training. What works for Group A demotivates Group C. What C needs bores Group A. Personalize to multiply results.

Topic 1
1

⭐ What to teach Group A

Group A is yourstop performers. They already master the basics, they already sell well, they already know how to get around objections. Train them in the basics and waste their talent and irritate competent professionals.

Group A training schedule

  • ✓ Complex negotiations:large clients, long contracts, multiple decision makers
  • ✓ Upsell and cross-sell:how to increase the ticket without forcing
  • ✓ Peer mentoring:transform A into method multiplier
  • ✓ Case studies:analyze lost and won sales to extract learning

📊 The strategic role of Group A

Your best salesperson training others is worth more than any external consultant. He knows the product, the customer and the company culture.Turn A into coachesand you scale knowledge at no cost.

Topic 2
2

📊 What to teach Group B

Group B and where is thehigher training ROI. These are people who have potential but lack consistency. With the right method, many migrate to A in 60 days. The secret: script, repetition and follow-up.

Group B development plan

1

Week 1-2: Script in hand

Memorize and practice the complete script. Respond with the visible itinerary. Zero improvisation.

2

Week 3-4: Script in the head

Internalize the script. Respond without consulting. Maintain the structure naturally.

3

Week 5-8: Fine Tuning

Refine specific objections. Work on individual weaknesses. Celebrate evolution.

💡 Critical tip

Group B needsweekly monitoring. Not monthly. Weekly. A 15-minute one-on-one meeting per week to review metrics and adjust. Without this, they return to old habits.

Topic 3
3

🔄 What to teach Group C

Group C is the most delicate. They are people withperformance consistently below the minimum. Training needs to be intensive, focused on the absolute basics, and with a defined deadline for evaluation.

What to train

  • Posture and body language
  • Active listening (stop interrupting)
  • The 4 basic questions
  • Follow the script without deviating
  • Greet and say goodbye correctly

Signs that it won't work

  • Refusal to follow the script
  • Do not practice outside of training
  • Constant excuses
  • Zero evolution in 30 days
  • Negative attitude towards the process

30-day protocol for Group C

📋
Day 1-10
Intensive daily training
Assessment continues
📊
Day 11-20
Practice with supervision
Objective decision
✅
Day 21-30
Evolve or reposition

📊 The most difficult decision

If after 30 days of intensive training the person has not reached the minimum baseline,consider a different function. It's not cruelty - it's respect. Keeping someone where they don't perform hurts them and the entire team.

Topic 4
4

⚡ Intensity and format per profile

Each group needs adifferent load and shape. Give the same training to everyone and how to prescribe the same medicine for different diseases. Customization is what makes the program work.

Intensity matrix

Aspect Group A Group B Group C
Frequency Fortnightly Weekly Daily
Duration 30-45 min 30 min 15-20 min
Format Case study role play Guided repetition
Focus Advanced Consistency Fundamentals
Supervision Autonomous Accompanied Intensive

💡 Rule of thumb

The worse the performance, the shorter and more frequent the session should be. Group C cannot handle 1 hour of training - but can handle 15 minutes every day. And 15 minutes every day beats 1 hour a week.

Topic 5
5

⏳ Duration and expectations per profile

Setrealistic deadlinesIt is essential to not frustrate anyone. Each profile evolves at different speeds. The manager needs to calibrate expectations with the team and with himself.

Timeline by group

GROUP A 1-2 weeks for visible results

Rapid evolution: refinement and advanced skills

GROUP B 30-60 days for consistent results

Gradual evolution: internalization of the method and change of habit

GROUP C 30 days for final evaluation

Evolve to B or leave: decision based on data, not hope

📊 Communication of expectations

Communicate deadlines to each person individually. "João, in the next 30 days we will focus on X. My expectation is that you will achieve Y by day Z. I will monitor you closely and help you."Clarity generates commitment.
