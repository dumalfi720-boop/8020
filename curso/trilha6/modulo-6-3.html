MODULE 6.3

📆 The 7-day method in practice

A complete, day-by-day itinerary for training a salesperson from scratch. Each day builds on the previous one. In 7 days, the seller is ready to serve you promptly.

Main Content
Topic 1 - Day 1
1

❓ Day 1: Questions

On the first day, the salesperson learns and practices the4 fundamental diagnostic questions. Questions are the basis of everything. A salesperson who asks questions well is already 70% of the way to the sale.

The 4 fundamental questions

Question 1 - Situation:

"How does your [process/product/service] work today?"

Question 2 - Problem:

"What bothers you most about this situation?"

Question 3 - Impact:

"How much does this cost you per month? (time, money, stress)"

Question 4 - Ideal solution:

"If you could solve this, what would the ideal scenario look like?"

💡 Day 1 Exercise

The salesperson practices the 4 questions in 5 consecutive simulations with colleagues. At the end, you should be able to ask the 4 questionswithout consulting the paper. Save the last simulation for review.

Topic 2 - Day 2
2

💔 Day 2: Pain and desire

On the second day, the salesperson learns toidentify and deepen the customer’s pain and desire. Every purchase is motivated by escaping a pain or pursuing a desire. Whoever masters this dominates the sale.

💔 Pain (run away from)

  • "I'm losing customers"
  • "I spend too much on this"
  • "I can't sleep because I'm worried"
  • "My competitors are ahead"

⭐ I want (search)

  • "I want to grow 30% this year"
  • "I need more free time"
  • "I want to be a reference in the market"
  • "My dream is to expand to 3 stores"

Deepening technique

When the client mentions a pain or desire, use these phrases to elaborate:

  • →"Tell me more about this..."
  • →"And how does this impact your day to day life?"
  • →"How long have you been living with this problem?"
  • →"If nothing changes, what will it be like in 6 months?"

💡 Day 2 Exercise

In pairs, one acts as the customer and the other as the seller. The seller must identify at least2 pains and 1 desirein 5 minutes of conversation. Then, exchange roles. Write down the sentences that worked best.

Topic 3 - Day 3
3

💎 Day 3: Value

On the third day, the salesperson learns totranslate product characteristics into value for the customer. The difference between "you have 10GB" and "you'll never lose a file again" is the difference between losing and winning the sale.

Value translation formula

Feature → Benefit → Impact on pain/desire
"Delivery in 24 hours"
"You get paid quickly"
"Solve the problem you told me TOMORROW"
"24/7 support"
"There's always someone"
"Never again that anguish of not having an answer"

💡 Day 3 Exercise

List the 5 main features of your product. For each one, write the benefit AND the impact on the customer's pain/desire.Practice out loud until it sounds natural, not decorated.

Topic 4 - Day 4
4

📝 Day 4: Script

On the fourth day, the seller puts everything together into onecomplete service script. It's not meant to be robotic - it's a framework that ensures no important steps are skipped.

Complete script structure

1

Opening (30s)

Greeting, name, quick rapport. "Hi, how are you? I'm [name]. Before I show you anything, I want to better understand your situation."

2

Diagnosis (3-5 min)

The 4 questions of Day 1. Listen more than talk. Write down pain and desire.

3

Value proposition (2-3 min)

Connect the solution to the identified pain/desire. Use the formula from Day 3. "Based on what you told me..."

4

Closing (1-2 min)

"Does this make sense to you? Can we start with [smaller option] or do you prefer [full option]?"

💡 Day 4 Exercise

Perform 3 complete simulations following the script from start to finish. At first, with the paper in hand. In the second, just watch when it brakes. In the third,of memory. Record the third.

Topic 5 - Day 5
5

🛡️ Day 5: Objections

On the fifth day, the salesperson memorizes and practices answers to the5 most common objections. 80% of lost sales die in predictable objections. If the seller already has the answer, don't freeze.

The 5 objections and their answers

Objection 1: "It's expensive"

Answer: "I understand. Let me ask you: how much are you losing per month with the problem you told me about? If we solve this, the investment will pay for itself in [deadline]."

Objection 2: "I am going to think"

Answer: "Of course, it makes sense to think. But tell me: what exactly do you need to think about? What about the value, the moment, or are there any doubts that I can clarify now?"

Objection 3: "I need to talk to my partner"

Answer: "I understand perfectly. But if it were up to you alone, would it make sense? If so, I can prepare a summary for you to present to your partner. Or better yet, can I speak to both of you together?"

Objection 4: "I already have a supplier"

Answer: "It's good that you already have a solution. Tell me: from 0 to 10, how satisfied are you with it? [If < 8] What would make it reach 10? Because maybe we can fill that gap."

Objection 5: "I don't need it now"

Answer: "I understand. But you told me that [mentioned pain]. The more time passes, will this problem tend to get worse or better on its own? If it gets worse, the cost of waiting may be greater than the cost of solving it now."

💡 Day 5 Exercise

Game of "rapid fire": a colleague throws random objections and the salesperson responds immediately.Repeat until responses come naturally in less than 3 seconds. Anyone who thinks too much goes back to the drawing board.

Topic 6 - Day 6
6

🎭 Day 6: Simulation

On the sixth day, the seller makes acomplete recorded simulation. This is the first time that it brings everything together: opening, diagnosis, value, script and objections in a single simulated service.

Day 6 simulation script

Step 1 Create a realistic scenario (use a real case from the company)
Step 2 Record the entire simulated service on video or audio
Step 3 Watch the recording with the seller (no judgment)
Step 4 Write down 3 strengths and 1 point for improvement
Step 5 Repeat the simulation applying the adjustment

📊 Why recording is so powerful

No one believes in their own addictions until they see it on the screen. The recording is a mirror that does not lie. The salesperson notices nonsense, inappropriate tone of voice, moments of hesitation and missed opportunities thatI would never notice otherwise.

Topic 7 - Day 7
7

🚀 Day 7: Real service

The big day. The seller answers areal client with supervision. The supervisor stays nearby (but without interfering) and then gives immediate feedback. And the transition from training to reality.

Day 7 Protocol

  • ✓ Before:review the script for 5 minutes. Breathe. Remember that making mistakes is part of the process.
  • ✓ During:supervisor watches without speaking. Write down points on the checklist. It doesn't correct live.
  • ✓ After:immediate feedback (maximum 10 minutes later). 3 compliments + 1 adjustment.
  • ✓ Repetition:serve 2-3 more customers on the same day with the correction applied.

Supervisor checklist

☐ Correct opening
☐ Asked all 4 questions
☐ Identified pain/desire
☐ Presented connected value
☐ Avoided objection
☐ Tried closing

💡 What to expect

On Day 7, the seller will not be perfect. It's impossible. The objective is that hefollow the structure, even if it stumbles at times. Perfection comes with repetition in the following weeks. The important thing is that the method is there.
