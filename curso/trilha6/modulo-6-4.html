🎭 Simulation and role play

Simulation is the most powerful and most underutilized training tool. Master the technique of setting up scenarios, conducting role plays and giving feedback that transforms.

🧪 Why simulation works

Neuroscience explains: when yousimulates a situation, activates the same brain areasof the real situation. The brain does not completely differentiate between training and reality. That's why athletes visualize the test before competing. That's why pilots train in simulators. That's why sellers need role play.

Why simulation trumps theory

🧠
Muscle memory
The body learns to react automatically
⚡
Anxiety reduction
Familiarity reduces fear of the unknown
🔄
Risk-free repetition
Making mistakes in training doesn’t cost customers
📈
Immediate feedback
Correction occurs at the time of the error

📊 The data that convinces

Studies show that people who practice through simulation retain75% of the contentafter 30 days. Anyone who just listens to a lecture retains 5%. Whoever retains 10%. The difference is brutal and unquestionable.

Topic 2
2

🏗️ How to create real scenarios

Simulation scenarios need to bebased on real situationsthat the team faces on a daily basis. Generic scenarios generate generic training. Use real consultations, recordings, complaints and real objections.

Simulation scenario template

CUSTOMER PROFILE:

[Describe who the client is: businessman, housewife, freelance professional...]

SITUATION:

[What the customer is looking for and why they got in touch]

MAIN PAIN:

[What bothers the customer most about the current situation]

LIKELY OBJECTION:

[What objection should the "client" present during the simulation]

DIFFICULTY LEVEL:

[Easy / Medium / Hard]

💡 Scenario bank

Create a bank with at least10 different scenariosbased on the most common types of customers. Update monthly with new situations. This way you never run out of training material.

Topic 3
3

🎪 Roles in the simulation

A good simulation hasthree distinct roles, and each one develops different skills. Rotating roles ensures everyone learns from all perspectives.

🗣️

Seller

Practice complete service. Apply script, questions, value and outline of objections.

Develops: execution
🎭

Customer

Simulates real behaviors: objections, hesitations, difficult questions. Follow the defined scenario.

Develops: empathy
👁️

Observer

Note strengths and weaknesses using the checklist. It doesn't interfere. Then, give structured feedback.

Develop: critical analysis

📊 Optimal rotation

In a 30-minute session with 3 people, each person plays each role once (3 rounds of ~10 min). In a week,each salesperson will have practiced the 3 roles at least 3 times. This generates 360-degree learning.

📋 How to evaluate the simulation

Subjective evaluation ("it was good", "it improved") doesn't teach anything. Do you need onechecklist with objective criteriathat anyone can use to evaluate any simulation consistently.

Simulation assessment checklist

1. Made a cordial and professional opening Y/N
2. Asked the 4 diagnostic questions Y/N
3. Listened more than you spoke Y/N
4. Identified at least 1 pain or desire Y/N
5. Presented value connected to pain/desire Y/N
6. Overcame objection with appropriate technique Y/N
7. Tried closure or next step Y/N
8. Appropriate and safe tone of voice Y/N
NOTICE: ___/8 | Minimum target: 6/8

💡 Golden tip

Use the same checklist every week. Note the evolution. In 4 weeks, you will have aclear progress graphfrom each seller. This motivates those who are improving and identifies those who need more attention.

Topic 5
5

💬 Feedback that changes behavior

The difference between feedback that transforms and feedback that destroys is intechnique, not intention. Many managers want to help but end up becoming unmotivated because they don't know how to give feedback. This can be learned.

The effective feedback formula

1

Praise what worked

"I really liked how you asked the question about the problem. The client opened his guard immediately."

2

Point out ONE point for improvement

"One thing that could improve: when he said 'it's expensive', you were silent. Try using the response we trained."

3

Show HOW to improve

"Next time, when you hear 'it's expensive,' try saying, 'I understand. How much are you losing per month with this problem?' Shall we practice?"

✓ Feedback that works

  • "You did X very well"
  • "One thing to improve: Y"
  • "Try to do it like this: [demonstrates]"
  • "I'm seeing real evolution"

✗ Feedback that destroys

  • "Everything went wrong again"
  • "I've already explained it a thousand times"
  • "You're hopeless"
  • "Why don't you do like João?"

💡 Golden Rule of Feedback

Never more thanone point of improvement at a time. The brain does not process multiple corrections simultaneously. Improving one thing per session is 52 improvements in a year. It transforms anyone.
