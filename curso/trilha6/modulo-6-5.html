📊 What to measure

Not everything that can be measured matters. And not everything that matters can be easily measured. The secret is choosingright indicators: few, relevant and easy to collect.

The 6 essential indicators

🎯
conversion rate
% of calls that turn into sales
💰
Average ticket
Average value per sale
❓
Questions asked
% of consultations with diagnosis
🛡️
Outline of objections
% of objections bypassed
⭐
Customer satisfaction
NPS or post-service rating
📋
Note in the simulation
Score on the weekly checklist

💡 Simplifying

If you can only measure ONE thing, measureconversation rate. If you can measure two, add themedium ticket. These two metrics together tell 80% of the story.

👥 Metrics per profile

Comparing a salesperson from Group C with one from Group A is unfair and counterproductive. Each profile needscalibrated goalsthat reflect your starting point and realistic potential.

Goals by group

Metric Group A Group B Group C
Conversion +5-10% +20-40% Reach baseline
Average ticket +10-15% +5-10% To maintain
Diagnosis 100% 80%+ 50%+
Score simulation 8/8 6/8+ 4/8+
Term 2 weeks 60 days 30 days

📊 The most motivating metric

Don't compare sellers to each other. Compareeach seller with himself from last month. "You were at 3/8 and now you're at 5/8. That's a 67% improvement!" This motivates more than any ranking.

✅ How to know if training works

Sales results may take weeks to appear. But there areantecedent signswhich show that the training is working even before the numbers change.

Signs that it's working

  • ✓ Salespeople use diagnostic questionsspontaneously
  • ✓ Objections are no longer a reason for panic
  • ✓ Customers praise service without being asked
  • ✓ Score in simulations rises week by week
  • ✓ The team asks for more training instead of avoiding

Signs that it is NOT working

  • ✗ Metrics stagnant for more than 3 weeks
  • ✗ Team constantly complains about training
  • ✗ Salespeople return to their old ways on Monday
  • ✗ Score in simulations does not evolve

💡 Strategic patience

At least3 weeksbefore judging whether the program works. Behavioral change is not instantaneous. But if in 3 weeks there is no positive sign, adjust the program - don't persist in what doesn't work.

📊 The force of visibility

When the numbers are visible to everyone, the team regulates itself. Whoever is moving up wants to maintain it. Whoever is standing still wants to go up.Transparency is the best management tool. Update every Friday.

Topic 5
5

🔧 When to adjust the program

A training program is not static. He needsevolve with the team and the market. But changing too much too soon is just as bad as never changing at all. Use objective triggers to decide.

Adjustment triggers

!

Metrics stagnated for 2+ weeks

The content may be disconnected from real needs. Review the scenarios and themes.

!

Team unmotivated with the format

Change the dynamics: swap role play for case study, or vice versa. Keep the content, change the format.

!

New product or change in the market

Update scenarios, scripts and objection responses. Training needs to reflect current reality.

!

Team dominated the current content

Time to advance to the next level. Don't repeat what has already been internalized. Challenge with new themes.

💡 Review cycle

Review the program formallyonce a month. Ask yourself: are the metrics evolving? Does the format still engage? Is the content relevant? If two of those answers are “no,” it’s time to adjust.
