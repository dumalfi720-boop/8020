Continuous vs punctual training

Punctual training is how to take medicine once and hope to be cured forever. Continuous training is like physical exercise — it only works with consistency. Here you understand why and how to turn training into a habit.

4 Topics ~30 min Execution Culture
Topic 1
📉

1. Why training once doesn’t solve the problem

In 1885, psychologist Hermann Ebbinghaus demonstrated something that every manager needs to understand: without reinforcement, human beings forget most of what they learned in a matter of hours. One-off training without follow-up and literally throwing money away.

The Ebbinghaus forgetting curve

Immediately after training 100%
After 20 minutes 58%
After 1 hour 44%
After 24 hours 33%
After 6 days 25%
After 31 days 21%

In 24 hours, your salesperson has already lost around 70% of what they learned in training. In a month, only 21% remains.

Spot training

  • ✗ 8-hour workshop once a year
  • ✗ Massive information without practice
  • ✗ No follow-up
  • ✗ Retention: ~20% after 30 days
  • ✗ High cost, low return

Continuous training

  • ✓ Daily/weekly micro-training
  • ✓ Small doses with immediate practice
  • ✓ Constant reinforcement of learning
  • ✓ Retention: 80%+ with spaced repetition
  • ✓ Low cost, exponential return
Topic 2
⏰

2. Daily microtraining

15 minutes a day. And that's it. No need for a room, projector, coffee break or external speaker. It needs intention and consistency. Three simple formats that transform the team without stopping the operation.

1

One objection a day

Choose a real objection that appeared the day before. In 15 minutes: present the objection (2 min), each salesperson writes their response (5 min), discuss the best ones (5 min), choose the standard response (3 min).

Monday example:"The competitor is cheaper." → Each person writes how they would answer → Compare → Best answer becomes part of the repertoire.
2

A quick role play

Two people, 5 minutes of simulation, 10 minutes of feedback. Scenario from the previous day. It doesn't have to be perfect — it needs to be frequent. The more you practice, the more natural it becomes.

Example from Tuesday:Simulate opening a call with a cold lead. One plays the salesperson, the other plays the skeptical lead. Team feedback: what worked, what to improve.
3

A link review

Listen together to 5-10 minutes of a real call (with permission). Analyze: what went well, what could be improved, what was the decisive moment. Learning from a real case is 5x more effective than theory.

Wednesday example:Listen to the first 5 minutes of João's call with client X. Focus: how did he make the diagnosis? Did you use the right questions? Did the client open up?

Why 15 minutes beats 8 hours

per week (15 min x 5 days)
vs 8h of one-off course

vs 21% of one-off training

3. Ideal cadence

Daily microtraining is the basis. But the complete cadence has three cycles: weekly, fortnightly and monthly. Each cycle fulfills a different function — practice, adjustment and evaluation.

Training calendar template

WEEKLY Team simulation — every Friday, 30 min

Collective practice with real scenario. Rotate papers. Immediate feedback. Close with a key learning that everyone applies the following week.

Template: Scenario of the week → Simulation (10 min) → Collective feedback (15 min) → Commitment of the week (5 min)
FORTWEEKLY Results review and adjustment — 45 min

Look at the numbers from the last 2 weeks. What changed? Have training commitments been enforced? What impact did it have on the results? Adjust the focus of the next cycle.

Template: Period metrics → Evolution analysis → What worked in training → What needs to change → New focus for the next 2 weeks
MONTHLY Evolution assessment by profile — 60 min

Individual assessment: how each salesperson evolved over the month. Compare metrics, adherence to the process, quality of interactions. Define individual development plan for the next month.

Template: Evolution by salesperson → Comparison with goal → Strengths of the month → Development point → Plan for the next month

Consolidated monthly view

Week Diary (15 min) Weekly (30 min) Extra
Week 1 Objection + Role play + Review Team simulation —
Week 2 Objection + Role play + Review Team simulation + Biweekly review
Week 3 Objection + Role play + Review Team simulation —
Week 4 Objection + Role play + Review Team simulation + Monthly evaluation

Total investment: ~7 hours/month per salesperson. Return: team that continually improves and does not depend on one-off courses.

Topic 4
🏛️

4. Training culture

The ultimate goal is not to have a training program — it is to have a culture where training is as natural as breathing. When the team asks for training instead of running away from it, you win. Here is the way to build this in 30 days.

From obligation to habit: 4 stages

1
Endurance (Week 1-2)

"I don't have time for that." "I already know how to sell." "That's a waste of time." Normal. The team resists because they never really trained. Persist. Keep the 15 minutes short and relevant. Use real cases of them — not abstract theory.

2
Acceptance (Week 2-3)

"It's not that bad." Resistance decreases when they realize that it is quick, practical and about their reality. They begin to participate without complaining. They still don't ask — but they don't run away.

3
Engagement (Week 3-4)

"Yesterday I used that technique and it worked!" The magic moment. When training generates real results, the team connects cause and effect. They start bringing cases to discuss. Training becomes part of the day.

4
Culture (Month 2 onwards)

"Shall we practice that new objection?" The team asks for training. Bring scenarios. Suggest themes. The manager no longer needs to "push" — the culture sustains itself. New sellers are already getting into this rhythm.

Signs of maturity — how to know it worked

▸ Salespeople bring objections they have heard to discuss in a group
▸ Ask for feedback after difficult calls
▸ They practice among themselves without the manager asking
▸ Share techniques that worked
▸ New salespeople are trained by their own colleagues
▸ The team complains when training is canceled

30-day plan to build workout culture

Days 1-7 Start with 15 min/day of microtraining. Use only real team cases. Don't explain the methodology — just do it. Celebrate any participation.
Days 8-14 Add weekly simulation (Friday, 30 min). Ask someone on the team to bring the scenario. Start connecting training with results: "João, did that technique we trained generate closure?"
Days 15-21 Ask salespeople to lead micro-training. When someone on the team teaches, engagement soars. Start documenting best practices as you emerge.
Days 22-30 Carry out the first monthly assessment. Show the evolution of each one. Celebrate progress. Ask the team: "What do you want to train next month?" Culture is being born.
