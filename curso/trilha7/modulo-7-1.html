🎯 The Complete 20 Questions

The questionnaire is divided into4 blocks of 5 questionseach. Each block assesses a fundamental dimension of the commercial profile: aptitude, desire, learning and execution. Together, the 20 questions create the complete salesperson map.

Block 1: Aptitude

Block 1: APTITUDE (Natural Ability)

Assesses the innate ability to communicate and influence in commercial contexts.

A1
Do you quickly create connections with new people?

Assesses natural rapport. 1 = hangs with strangers, 5 = connects in seconds.

A2
Can you hold a conversation confidently and fluently?

Evaluates verbal communication. 1 = loses the thread, 5 = articulates clearly and confidently.

A3
Do you actively listen and notice customer signals?

Assesses active listening. 1 = speaks more than listens, 5 = captures nuances and between the lines.

A4
Do you argue naturally without seeming forced?

Evaluates power of persuasion. 1 = looks like a memorized script, 5 = convinces naturally.

A5
Does it convey trust and credibility?

Assess presence. 1 = insecure and hesitant, 5 = immediately inspires confidence.

Block 2: Will

Block 2: WILL (Internal Motivation)

Assesses the genuine desire to work in the commercial area and emotional resilience.

V1
Do you like negotiating and interacting with customers?

Evaluates affinity with sales. 1 = avoids contact, 5 = energized by business conversations.

V2
Meet the goal and the pressure without complaining?

Assess pressure tolerance. 1 = falls apart under pressure, 5 = works best under pressure.

V3
Do you handle rejection and “no” well?

Assess resilience. 1 = takes it personally, 5 = processes it quickly and moves on.

V4
Actively looking for opportunities?

Evaluates proactivity. 1 = waits for the lead to arrive, 5 = hunts for opportunities all the time.

V5
Do you genuinely want to improve in sales?

Assess growth ambition. 1 = accommodated, 5 = constantly seeks to learn and evolve.

Block 3: Learning

Block 3: LEARNING (Ability to Evolve)

Assesses the speed and willingness to absorb knowledge and change behavior.

P1
Do you accept feedback without defending yourself?

Evaluates coachability. 1 = justifies everything, 5 = listens, takes notes and thanks.

P2
Correct quickly after identifying an error?

Evaluates correction speed. 1 = repeats the same error, 5 = corrects it on the next attempt.

P3
Do you learn by watching the best on the team?

Assesses social learning. 1 = ignores references, 5 = studies and replicates what works.

P4
Do you test new approaches on your own?

Evaluate experimentation. 1 = always does the same thing, 5 = tests variations and measures results.

P5
Does it visibly evolve from one week to the next?

Assess learning curve. 1 = stagnant for months, 5 = notable improvement each week.

Block 4: Execution

Block 4: EXECUTION (Discipline and Process)

Assesses the ability to transform knowledge into consistent, daily action.

E1
Do you follow up consistently?

Evaluates persistence. 1 = forget after first contact, 5 = continue until closure or discard.

E2
Do you maintain a daily business routine?

Evaluates discipline. 1 = entire days without activity, 5 = predictable and followed routine.

E3
Do you use CRM/process in an organized way?

Evaluate organization. 1 = does not record anything, 5 = pipeline updated and organized.

E4
Does it meet the minimum commercial activity?

Evaluate volume. 1 = well below activity target, 5 = consistently exceeds target.

E5
Does it turn training into practical action?

Evaluate application. 1 = watch and forget, 5 = implement at the first opportunity.

📊 How to Score

Each question receives a grade of1 to 5. The score should reflect actual, observable behavior, not the seller's intent or potential. If possible, use concrete evidence.

Scoring Scale

1
Never / Very weak

Does not demonstrate competence. Total or almost total absence of behavior.

2
Rarely / Weak

Demonstrates in isolated situations. Inconsistent and below-expected behavior.

3
Sometimes / Average

Demonstrates with moderate frequency. It oscillates between good and bad without a clear pattern.

4
Often / Good

It shows in most situations. Behavior consistent with occasional failures.

5
Always / Excellent

Demonstrates consistently. Solid behavior that can serve as a reference for others.

💡 Golden Rule of Punctuation

When in doubt between two notes, choose the onelowest. It is better to identify a gap and discover that it does not exist than to ignore a real gap. Assessment is a development tool, not praise.

🏷️ How to Sort

After adding up the points for each block (5 questions x maximum score 5 =maximum 25 points per block), classify each dimension:

21 to 25 points

HIGH

Solid competence. Maintain and refine. Can be a reference for other salespeople on the team.

14 to 20 points

AVERAGE

Room for growth. Prioritize training and close monitoring in this dimension.

5 to 13 points

LOW

Critical gap. Urgent intervention required. Assess whether it is trainable or whether it is a structural limitation.

Practical Classification Example

Seller Fitness Willing Apprentice. Execution
Carlos 22 High 24 High 16 Media 11 Low
Dwarf 18 Media 23 High 21 High 22 High

Carlos has talent and desire but he doesn't execute — he needs routine monitoring, not technique training. Ana is consistent and trainable — investing in her pays off quickly.

Topic 4
4

📈 Commercial Fit = Media of the 4 Blocks

O Fit CommercialIt is the only indicator that summarizes commercial potential. Calculate the average of the 4 blocks and convert to a scale of 0 to 10. Formula: (sum of 4 blocks / 100) x 10.

8.5 to 10.0

FIT STRONG

Complete seller. Invest in bigger challenges, aggressive goals and a leadership position. High return on investment.

7.0 to 8.4

GOOD FIT

Solid base with points for improvement. Invest in targeted training on gaps. Potential to become a top performer.

5.5 to 6.9

PARTIAL FIT

Significant gaps. Assess whether the gaps are trainable. If yes, invest with a defined period. If not, consider relocation.

Below 5.5

FIT LOW

Misalignment with commercial function. Honest conversation necessary. It may be excellent in another area, but not in sales.

Calculation Example

Seller: Carlos

Aptitude: 22 | Will: 24 | Learning: 16 | Execution: 11

Total: 22 + 24 + 16 + 11 = 73

Commercial Fit: (73 / 100) x 10 = 7.3 → GOOD FIT

But be careful: Fit Bom hides a critical gap in Execution (11 = Low).

The average alone doesn't tell the full story — always look at the individual blocks.
