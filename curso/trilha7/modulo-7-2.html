Topic 1

👋 Opening Script

The opening sets the tone for the entire conversation. The objective is not to sell — andcreate connection and gain permission to continue. The more natural, the better.

Opening Script — Base Template

STEP 1 — Presentation with context

"Hi [name], how are you? I'm [your name], from [company]. I saw that you [context: filled out the form / participated in the event / were nominated by X]."

STEP 2 — Honest statement of intent

"I'm not going to take up too much of your time. I'd like to understand a little more about [the client's area] to see if it makes sense for us to talk."

STEP 3 — Permission question

"Can I ask you a few quick questions to better understand your situation?"

✓ What to do

  • Use the customer's name
  • Give context on how you got there
  • Be transparent about the reason for contact
  • Ask permission before delving deeper

✗ What to avoid

  • "I have an incredible opportunity"
  • Talk about the product in the first 60 seconds
  • Monologue about the company
  • Telemarketing tone or robotic script

2

🔍 Diagnostic Script

Diagnosis is the most important stage of the sale. They are4 questions in natural sequencethat reveal the customer’s situation, problem, impact and need.

The 4 Diagnostic Questions

1 SITUATION QUESTION

"How does [process/area] work in your company today?"

Objective: Understand the current scenario without judging. Let the customer speak.

2 PROBLEM question

"And what bothers you most about this process today? What isn't working as it should?"

Objective: Make the client verbalize the pain. The pain in his mouth is worth 10x more than in yours.

3 IMPACT Question

"And what has this impacted? In revenue? In time? In the team?"

Objective: Quantify pain. Transform a vague inconvenience into concrete damage.

4 NEED Question

"If you could solve this, what would the ideal scenario look like for you?"

Objective: Make the customer describe the solution in their own words. This becomes your proposal.

💡 Golden Rule of Diagnosis

During the diagnosis, speak as much as possible30% of the time. The other 70% should be the customer speaking. Your job is to ask questions and listen, not present. Write down the client's exact words — you will use them in the presentation.

💎 Value Presentation Script

Value presentation uses the model"From what you told me..."that directly connects the diagnosis to your solution. The client hears their own words and feels that the proposal was tailor-made.

Value Presentation Model

STEP 1 — Mirror the diagnosis

"From what you told me, today the main challenge is [repeating the problem using the customer's words], and this is causing [repeating the impact]."

STEP 2 — Validate the ideal scenario

"You told me that the ideal would be to [repeat the need expressed by the customer]. Does that make sense?"

STEP 3 — Connect the solution

"That's exactly what we do. The [product/service] solves [problem] because [mechanism]. In practice, customers like you achieve [concrete result]."

STEP 4 — Social proof

"[Similar client] had exactly this problem and within [time frame] he got [result]. Can I show you how?"

📊 Why this model works

The customer isn’t listening to a generic pitch — they’re listeningthe solution to YOUR problemdescribed in YOUR words. This eliminates the feeling of "they're trying to sell me something" and creates the feeling of "they understand my situation."

Topic 4
4

🤝 Closing Script

Closing is not a moment of pressure — and thenatural continuation of the conversation. If the diagnosis and presentation were done well, closure is only the next logical step.

3 Closing Techniques

Technique 1: Assumed Alternative

"Given your situation, I would recommend [plan A] or [plan B]. Which one makes more sense for you?"

When to use: When the customer has already shown clear interest. Offers options instead of yes/no.

Technique 2: Commitment Question

"If I show you that we solve [specific problem], would you be willing to start [deadline]?"

When to use: When the customer still has a specific doubt. Closing is conditional on resolving this doubt.

Technique 3: Next Steps

"Great, so the next step would be [concrete action]. Can I send you [proposal/contract] today and we can start [date]?"

When to use: When the conversation flows well and there is clear alignment. Move to action without asking “so what?”.

💡 Closure Principle

After asking the closing question,stay silent. Whoever speaks first after the closing question loses. The silence is uncomfortable, but it is where the decision happens. Resist the temptation to complete the sentence or give a discount.

🔄 Variations by Segment

The base script works for any context, butsmall adaptations per segmentthey make a big difference in the naturalness and effectiveness of the conversation.

Services (Intangibles)

→
Opening: Focus on results, not on the description of the service. "We help companies like yours [result]."
→
Diagnosis: Ask about current processes and results obtained. "How much time do you spend on this today?"
→
Value: Use concrete cases with numbers. "Customer X saved Y hours per month."
→
Closing: Offer pilot or test. "How about we start with a 30-day pilot project?"

Products (Tangibles)

→
Opening: Connect the product to a need. "I noticed that you [situation] — we have something that can make it easier."
→
Diagnosis: Focus on current use and dissatisfaction. "What do you wear today? What do you wish was different?"
→
Value: Demonstration and comparison. Show the product in action and compare it to alternatives.
→
Closing: Direct for experimentation. "Do you want to test it before deciding?"

B2B (Business to Business)

→
Opening: Research the company first. "I saw that the [company] is growing in [area]. How is the [process]?"
→
Diagnosis: Focus on business metrics. "What is the [KPI] target? Where is the bottleneck?"
→
Value: ROI and business case. "For every R$1 invested, customers like you recover R$X."
→
Closing: Map the decision process. "Besides you, who else participates in this decision?"

B2C (Business to Consumer)

→
Opening: Lighter and more personal tone. "What brought you here today? What are you looking for?"
→
Diagnosis: Focus on emotion and desire. "What would change in your life if you resolved this?"
→
Value: Stories and testimonials. "The [person] was in the same situation and today [result]."
→
Closing: Make the decision easier. "We can start today. What do you prefer, [option A] or [option B]?"
