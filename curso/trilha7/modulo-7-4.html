📅 Weekly Checklist

The weekly routine andmanager operating system. Sticking to this list every week ensures that no problem grows unnoticed.

Manager's Weekly Checklist

☐
Pipeline meeting (Monday)

Review all deals in progress. Ask: what is the next step? When? What's left to close? Remove dead deals.

☐
Listen to 2-3 calls/recordings per salesperson

You don't need to hear everything. Listen for opening and closing. Note specific points of improvement for feedback.

☐
One-on-one with each salesperson (15-20 min)

Ask: how are you? What is stopping? What do you need from me? Give specific feedback based on listening.

☐
Review team activity metrics

Calls made, meetings scheduled, proposals sent, follow-ups carried out. Who is below the activity target?

☐
Plan the following week (Friday)

Set priorities, prepare agendas, identify who needs special attention. Never start Monday without a plan.

📆 Monthly Checklist

The monthly cycle and where the manager does itstrategic adjustments. It's the highest-level vision that connects daily activity with long-term results.

Manager's Monthly Checklist

☐
Analysis of results vs goals

Did each salesperson reach the target? If not, why? Was it lack of activity, conversion rate or average ticket? Identify root cause.

☐
Individual evaluation of each seller

Use the Commercial Diagnosis (Module 7.1) as a reference. Compare with the previous month. Has there been evolution?

☐
Review of processes and tools

Is CRM being used? Are the scripts updated? Do any processes need adjustment? Any new and necessary tools?

☐
Meeting results with the team

Present numbers, recognize highlights, point out collective gaps. Constructive tone, not punitive. Celebrate victories.

☐
Planning for the following month

Define goals, training topics, improvement actions, and necessary resources. Align with the company's direction.

👀 What to Look for in the Team

Numbers show the past.Behavior shows the future. The manager who observes early signs acts before results fall.

🚨 Warning Signs

  • ! Drop in the volume of activities without justification
  • ! Delays in follow-ups and responses
  • ! Outdated or abandoned CRM
  • ! Repeated excuses for low results
  • ! Isolation — does not participate in meetings, avoids feedback
  • ! Frequent conflicts with clients or colleagues
  • ! Pipeline stagnant — no new deals coming in

✓ Signs of Progress

  • ✓ Increasing or stable volume of activity
  • ✓ Question about best practices — seeks to evolve
  • ✓ Apply feedback received in the next interaction
  • ✓ Healthy pipeline with deals at different stages
  • ✓ Share learnings with the team
  • ✓ Conversion rate improving month after month
  • ✓ Increasing autonomy — solve more without needing a manager

🎯 What to Fix First

The correction order matters. Correcting in the wrong sequence wastes time and frustrates everyone. Follow thehierarchy of priorities:

Commercial Correction Pyramid

1

ACTIVITY VOLUME

Is the seller doing enough activity? If you don't make a call, don't schedule a meeting, don't send a proposal — nothing else matters. Fix it FIRST.

Diagnosis: how many calls/day? How many meetings/week? How many proposals/month?

2

QUALITY OF THE APPROACH

Does activity but doesn’t convert? The approach is probably weak. Listen to calls, review emails, correct scripts.

Diagnosis: call-to-meeting conversion rate? Customer feedback?

3

PIPELINE MANAGEMENT

Schedule meetings but deals don't move forward? Problem with follow-up, prioritization or funnel management. Teach how to manage opportunities.

Diagnosis: average time in the funnel? Deals stopped for more than X days? No-show fee?

4

CLOSING TECHNIQUE

It reaches the end but doesn't close? Oh yes — training closing techniques, handling objections, negotiation.

Diagnosis: how many proposals turned into sales? What is the main objection heard?

💡 Classic Error

The manager sees a low result and sends the seller to a “negotiation technique” course. But the problem was that he did3 calls a day instead of 15. No technique saves those who don't have volume. Always start at level 1.

📋 Manager Routine Template

A model agenda withdistribution of activities by day of the week. Adapt to your context, but maintain the structure.

COMMERCIAL MANAGER’S WEEKLY AGENDA

MONDAY — Pipeline Day

08:00-09:00 — Review metrics from the previous week

09:00-10:30 — Pipeline meeting with team

10:30-12:00 — One-on-one with salespeople (2-3)

14:00-16:00 — Listen to recordings/follow live sales

16:00-17:00 — Note feedback and plan interventions

TUESDAY — Development Day

08:00-09:00 — Prepare training/training for the week

09:00-10:30 — Team training session

10:30-12:00 — One-on-one with salespeople (2-3)

14:00-16:00 — Individual coaching with salesperson in gap

16:00-17:00 — Update POIs

WEDNESDAY — Follow-up Day

08:00-10:00 — Monitor live sales / shadow meetings

10:00-12:00 — Respond to schedules and help with negotiations

14:00-16:00 — Review CRM and quality of records

16:00-17:00 — Immediate feedback on the day’s follow-ups

THURSDAY — Strategic Day

08:00-10:00 — Data analysis and trends

10:00-12:00 — Meeting with direction/strategic alignment

14:00-16:00 — Planning improvement actions

16:00-17:00 — Create/update supporting materials

FRIDAY — Closing and Planning Day

08:00-10:00 — Support pending closures

10:00-11:00 — Quick meeting of the week's results

11:00-12:00 — Recognize highlights of the week

14:00-16:00 — Plan next week

16:00-17:00 — Self-assessment: what did I do well? What do I need to improve?
